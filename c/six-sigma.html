六西格玛 liù xīgémǎSix Sigma

🔊 Six Sigma /sɪks ˈsɪɡmə/

以数据与统计驱动的质量管理方法,目标是把缺陷率降到每百万次机会不超过3.4次（六个标准差）。核心流程 DMAIC:定义-测量-分析-改进-控制。它把质量从主观判断变成可测量、可控制的统计工程。

A data- and statistics-driven quality methodology aiming to cut defects to no more than 3.4 per million opportunities (six standard deviations). Its core process is DMAIC: Define, Measure, Analyse, Improve, Control. It turns quality from subjective judgement into measurable, controllable statistical engineering.

Etymology 词源

sigma（希腊字母 σ）是统计中标准差的符号——'六西格玛'即质量控制在正负六个标准差内,缺陷率极低;摩托罗拉与 GE 使其闻名。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ DMAIC /dəˈmeɪɪk/
    DMAIC gives improvement a disciplined, repeatable spine.
  • ▸ defect reduction /ˈdifɛkt rɪˈdəkʃən/
    Defect reduction at Six Sigma levels means near-perfect, measured to the decimal.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a Six Sigma black belt

In the wild 语境例句

Six Sigma attacks variation: by measuring and controlling every source of defect, a process approaches near-perfection instead of merely 'good enough'.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

批评:六西格玛擅长优化现有流程,却可能扼杀创新（过度控制变异也压制探索）——3M 引入后创新受挫是著名反例。
