曾国藩（结硬寨打呆仗与日课自省） Zēng GuófānZeng Guofan: dumb persistence and daily audit

⏳ 1811年–1872年

张宏杰《曾国藩传》：资质平平（考秀才七次）而成“立德立功立言三不朽”——秘诀是“尚拙”：结硬寨、打呆仗（湘军不求奇谋，深沟高垒步步为营困死太平军）；日课十二条与写日记自省（把修身工程化）；“大处着眼，小处着手”；晚年办洋务（江南制造局）开近代化先声。与斯多亚日省、富兰克林美德清单同型——中式自我管理的原典人物，管理界奉为“笨功夫”哲学。

Zhang Hongjie's bestselling life of the Qing statesman who failed the licentiate exam seven times and ended as the dynasty's model of the 'three immortalities': his method was engineered dullness — 'build hard camps, fight dumb battles' (the Xiang Army starved the Taiping out with trenches, not brilliance); a twelve-item daily regimen and a diary of merciless self-audit; 'plan from the large, act from the small'; and, late, the arsenals that began self-strengthening. Franklin's virtue ledger and the Stoic evening review, Chinese-style — business China's favourite patron saint of un-clever persistence.

In the wild 语境例句

“结硬寨，打呆仗” circulates in Chinese business writing exactly where English cites 'compound interest' and 'boring wins' — Buffett's moat rhetoric has a Hunanese ancestor.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“结硬寨打呆仗”可译 build hard camps, fight dull battles（需释：attritional discipline over cleverness）。三不朽 = the three immortalities (virtue, deeds, words——《左传》)。链 [[taiping]]（他是镇压者——同一史实的两个节点视角！）、[[self-strengthening]]（他是发起人）、[[stoicism]]、[[atomic-habits]]（日课=习惯系统）。
