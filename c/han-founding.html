Liu Bang founded the Han in 202 BC over a wasteland вҖ” the emperor could not match four horses of one colour, his ministers rode ox-carts. Learning from Qin's death-by-exertion, the early Han governed by 'rest and recuperation': soldiers sent home to farm, slaves freed, taxes cut to a fifteenth and then a thirtieth of the crop, mutilating punishments abolished, the palace kept frugal. Two generations of deliberate under-government filled the granaries вҖ” the Wenjing golden age, China's founding case for small-state economics.
