商誉与减值 shāngyù yǔ jiǎnzhígoodwill and impairment

🔊 goodwill impairment /ˈgʊdˈwɪl ˌɪmˈpɛrmənt/

并购中收购价超过被收购方可辨认净资产公允价值的部分记为“商誉”——本质是为协同与未来盈利支付的溢价。当该业务价值不及预期时须计提“减值”，一次性冲减利润，等于承认当初买贵了。

In an acquisition, the excess of the purchase price over the fair value of the target's identifiable net assets is booked as goodwill — essentially the premium paid for synergies and future earnings. When that business underperforms, an impairment charge writes it down, hitting profit in one blow — an admission that the deal was overpaid.

Etymology 词源

goodwill 本义“商誉、好感”——收购中指“愿为品牌、客户、协同等无形好处多付的钱”；impair 源自拉丁 pejorare（使更坏）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ a write-down /ə raɪt-daʊn/
    A write-down is management confessing, in accounting, that it once paid too much.
  • ▸ the impairment charge /ðə ˌɪmˈpɛrmənt ʧɑrʤ/
    The impairment charge is a past acquisition's overpayment coming home to roost.
