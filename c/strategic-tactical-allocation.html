🔊 strategic versus tactical allocation /strəˈtiʤɪk ˈvərsəz ˈtæktɪkəl ˌæləˈkeɪʃən/

战略资产配置(SAA)是依IPS设定的长期目标比例(政策组合);战术资产配置(TAA)是围绕它做的短期主动偏离,押注市场时机。再平衡则是定期把漂移的比例拉回目标——它天然'高抛低吸',是纪律化的风险控制。多数证据支持坚守战略配置+再平衡,慎用战术择时。

Strategic asset allocation (SAA) is the long-run target mix set by the IPS (the policy portfolio); tactical asset allocation (TAA) is short-term active deviation around it, betting on market timing. Rebalancing periodically pulls the drifted weights back to target — inherently 'sell high, buy low', a disciplined risk control. Most evidence favours holding the strategic allocation and rebalancing, and using tactical timing sparingly.
