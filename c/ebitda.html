🔊 EBITDA /ɪˈbɪtdɑː/

利润在扣除利息、税、折旧、摊销之前的水平——近似衡量核心经营的现金生成能力，且剔除资本结构与税制差异，便于跨公司比较。但它忽略维持业务所需的资本开支，重资产企业用它会系统性高估盈利。

Earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortisation — a rough proxy for the cash-generating power of core operations, stripping out capital-structure and tax differences for cross-company comparison. But it ignores the capex needed to sustain the business, systematically overstating earnings for asset-heavy firms.

Etymology 词源

Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortisation 缩略（读作 'ee-bit-dah'）——1980年代杠杆收购推动其流行。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ operating cash proxy /ˈɔpərˌeɪtɪŋ kæʃ ˈprɑksi/
    EBITDA is an operating cash proxy that forgets the factory needs replacing.
  • ▸ bullshit earnings /ˈbʊlˌʃɪt ˈərnɪŋz/
    Munger called EBITDA 'bullshit earnings' for pretending depreciation isn't a real cost.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the EBITDA margin
  • ▸ adjusted EBITDA
  • ▸ EBITDA less capex
  • ▸ turns of EBITDA

In the wild 语境例句

A cable company trumpets its EBITDA while its network devours capex every year — which is why free cash flow, not EBITDA, tells the honest story.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

'Adjusted EBITDA'（调整后 EBITDA）尤须警惕:管理层可把各种真实成本'调整'掉，粉饰盈利——芒格与巴菲特都视 EBITDA 为最易被滥用的指标。
