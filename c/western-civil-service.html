西方文官制度 Xīfāng wénguān zhìdùthe Western civil service

🔊 civil service /ˈsɪvəl ˈsərvɪs/

⏳ 1855年–1883年

近代西方原行恩赐官职制、“政党分肥制”（美国，selon胜选者分配官职），腐败低效。工业革命后国家管理职能急剧扩展，要求专业化官员队伍：英国1855、1870年两次枢密院令确立文官制度——公开考试、择优录用、政治中立、职务常任、论功晋升；美国1883年《彭德尔顿法》建立联邦文官制度。西方文官制度的建立借鉴了中国科举制；它规范了西方国家政府行政部门事务官员的选用管理、实现了政治和管理的分离、有利于政府工作稳定性持续性、促进了国家治理水平提高（亦有官僚习气、机构膨胀等问题）。

Before reform, Western offices went by patronage — America's 'spoils system' rotating the bureaucracy with each election — until industrial-age government outgrew amateurs: Britain's orders-in-council (1855, 1870) built the modern civil service on open competitive examination, merit, permanence and political neutrality, and America's Pendleton Act (1883) followed. The textbook states plainly what Victorian critics sneered at: the model borrowed from China's examinations. Politics separated from administration; the permanent government was born — with its own pathologies of bureaucratism and bloat duly noted.
