市盈率 shìyínglǜthe price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio

🔊 price-earnings ratio /praɪs-ˈərnɪŋz ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ/

股价除以每股盈利——投资者为每一元盈利愿付多少钱。高 P/E 反映高增长预期或低风险（或市场狂热）；低 P/E 或为价值机会或为价值陷阱。最普及也最易误用的估值指标。

Share price divided by earnings per share — how much investors pay for each dollar of profit. A high P/E reflects high growth expectations or low risk (or market exuberance); a low P/E may signal value or a value trap. The most popular and most misused valuation metric.

Etymology 词源

earnings 源自古英语 earnian（赚取、应得）——市盈率即“为每单位赚取所得付出的价格倍数”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the earnings multiple /ðə ˈərnɪŋz ˈməltəpəl/
    The earnings multiple prices a dollar of profit — richly for growth, cheaply for doubt.
  • ▸ the PEG ratio /ðə pɛg ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ/
    The PEG ratio divides the P/E by growth, so a high multiple can still look cheap.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ trade at 20x earnings
  • ▸ forward vs trailing P/E
  • ▸ a P/E re-rating
  • ▸ the earnings yield

In the wild 语境例句

A stock at 40x earnings is pricing in years of rapid growth — if growth stalls, the multiple compresses and the shares fall twice over.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

P/E 用股权价值与净利润，受杠杆与税率扭曲，且盈利可被会计操纵——比较不同资本结构的公司时不如 EV/EBITDA 干净。
