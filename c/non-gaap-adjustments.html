🔊 non-GAAP adjustments /nɑn-ɡæp əˈʤəstmənts/

公司在法定报表之外自行披露'调整后'指标(调整后EBITDA、经调整每股收益),剔除它们认为非经营或非现金的项目。监管要求与GAAP对账。它们可提供有用视角,也常被用来系统性美化盈利——分析师须逐项审视被'调整掉'的是什么、是否合理。

Beyond the statutory statements, companies disclose their own 'adjusted' metrics (adjusted EBITDA, adjusted EPS), excluding items they deem non-operating or non-cash. Regulators require reconciliation to GAAP. They can add useful perspective but are also used to systematically flatter profit — analysts must scrutinise item by item what was 'adjusted out' and whether it is legitimate.

Etymology 词源

GAAP 见前;non-GAAP 即'非官方准则'的自定义口径,须与 GAAP '对账'（reconciliation）披露。
