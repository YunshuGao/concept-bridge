并购 bìnggòumergers and acquisitions (M&A)

🔊 mergers and acquisitions /ˈmərʤərz ənd ˌækwəˈzɪʃənz/

企业通过合并或收购实现外延增长:进入新市场、获取能力技术、消除竞争、追求规模与协同。统计显示多数并购未能为收购方创造价值——赢家的诅咒、整合失败与过高溢价是主因。

Growing by combining with or buying other firms: to enter markets, acquire capabilities and technology, remove competitors, or pursue scale and synergies. Studies show most deals fail to create value for the acquirer — the winner's curse, botched integration and overpaid premiums are the usual culprits.

Etymology 词源

merge 源自拉丁 mergere（沉入、浸没）——合并即“两家沉入为一”；acquire 源自 acquirere（获取）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ dealmaking /ˈdilˌmeɪkɪŋ/
    Dealmaking is easy to start and hard to justify three years later.
  • ▸ inorganic growth /ˌɪnɔrˈgænɪk groʊθ/
    Inorganic growth buys tomorrow's revenue at today's premium.

In the wild 语境例句

Most M&A destroys value because the acquirer pays a premium for synergies that integration then fails to deliver — the acquirer's curse.
