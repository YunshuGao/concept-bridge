西周分封制 fēnfēngzhìthe Western Zhou enfeoffment system

also: the fengjian system · Zhou 'feudalism'

⏳ 前1046年–前771年

周王为稳定政局巩固疆土，据血缘远近与功劳大小分封宗亲功臣到各地建诸侯国，授予土地人民的管理权；诸侯独立性较大，但须进献贡物、服从调兵，且可在封地内再分封——确立“分封制”等级社会：天子、诸侯、卿大夫、士。（“我的附庸的附庸，不是我的附庸”式的层级在中国同样成立。）

To hold its new conquests the Zhou king parcelled the land among kinsmen and meritorious allies, each ruling a vassal state owing tribute and troops, each free to subinfeudate in turn — a graded order of king, lords, ministers and knights bound by blood and ritual. This fengjian ('enfeoffment') order is what the Chinese word 封建 originally meant.

In the wild 语境例句

Here is the pivot of the 封建 trap: Zhou enfeoffment genuinely resembles European feudalism — which is why calling imperial China after Qin 'feudal' is doubly wrong: the First Emperor's whole point was abolishing this system for governors he could fire.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

本知识库核心枢纽：封建 fengjian 的本义即此制，与欧洲 feudalism（[[lords-and-vassals]]）真正可比；而马克思主义分期把秦以后两千年称“封建社会”，与两者皆异——英语写作谈周制用 enfeoffment/the fengjian order，谈欧洲用 feudalism，谈帝制中国用 imperial China。三分清楚，误译绝迹。
