资本配置 zīběn pèizhìcapital allocation

🔊 capital allocation /ˈkæpɪtəl ˌæləˈkeɪʃən/

管理层决定把企业产生的现金投向何处的核心职责:再投资于核心、并购、偿债、分红或回购。巴菲特视之为 CEO 最重要却最被低估的技能——长期股东回报的差异，多源于资本配置的高下。

Management's core responsibility of deciding where the cash a business generates should go: reinvest in the core, acquire, pay down debt, pay dividends or buy back stock. Buffett calls it a CEO's most important yet most underrated skill — differences in long-run shareholder returns mostly trace to good or bad capital allocation.

Etymology 词源

allocate 源自拉丁 ad-（向）+ locare（放置，locus 位置）——配置即“把资本放到该放的位置”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the capital-allocation decision /ðə ˈkæpɪtəl-ˌæləˈkeɪʃən dɪˈsɪʒən/
    The capital-allocation decision is where a CEO's real philosophy shows, not the mission statement.
  • ▸ return on incremental capital /rɪˈtərn ɔn ˌɪnkrəˈmɛntəl ˈkæpɪtəl/
    Return on incremental capital tells you whether the next dollar reinvested was worth keeping.

In the wild 语境例句

Two identical businesses diverge over a decade purely on capital allocation — one compounds by reinvesting at high returns, the other squanders cash on empire-building.
