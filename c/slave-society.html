奴隶社会 núlì shèhuìslave society

also: slaveholding society · ancient/classical antiquity (Western usage)

⏳ 约前3500年起

马克思主义分期中继原始社会之后的阶段，以奴隶主占有奴隶和生产资料为基础；教材将古埃及、两河、印度、希腊罗马等早期文明归入此阶段。

In Marxist periodisation, the stage after primitive society, defined by masters owning slaves as the basis of production; Western historians instead speak simply of the ancient world or classical antiquity, reserving 'slave society' for cases like Athens, Rome or the American South where slavery structured the whole economy.

In the wild 语境例句

Historians still argue whether Rome was a slave society or merely a society with slaves — the distinction carries a whole theory of what made its economy run.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

陷阱：英语史学中 'slave society' 是描述性概念（奴隶制主导经济的社会），不是普遍历史阶段。翻译教材语境时可保留 slave society 并注明 Marxist periodisation；泛指上古时期用 antiquity/the ancient world 更自然。
