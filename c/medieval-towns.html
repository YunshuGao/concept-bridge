中世纪城市的自由与自治 zhōngshìjì chéngshìmedieval towns and urban self-government

also: free cities · chartered towns · communes

⏳ 10世纪–15世纪

10世纪起西欧农业恢复、商贸发展，旧城复苏新城涌现。城市多坐落于领主领地，居民以金钱赎买和武力斗争争取自由自治，13世纪许多城市从国王或领主取得“特许状”成为自由城市，部分可选市长、设城市法庭而自治；农奴入城住满一年零一天即获自由（“城市的空气使人自由”）。

As farming and trade revived from the 10th century, towns re-emerged on lords' lands and fought — with purse and pike — for liberty, winning royal or seigneurial charters that made them free, sometimes self-governing, cities; a serf who lived in a town for a year and a day was free, for 'town air makes you free'.

In the wild 语境例句

Special economic zones are the charter towns of our age — carve-outs where the sovereign trades control for growth, exactly the bargain medieval kings struck with their cities.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“特许状”= charter——该词至今核心：chartered company、charter school、the UN Charter。“城市的空气使人自由”源自德谚 Stadtluft macht frei，英译 town air makes (you) free。经济学家 Paul Romer 的 charter cities 概念直接借用此史。
