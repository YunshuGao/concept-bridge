股利折现模型 gǔlì zhéxiàn móxíngthe dividend discount model (DDM)

🔊 dividend discount model /ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd ˈdɪskaʊnt ˈmɑdəl/

最古老的股权估值法:股票价值等于其未来全部股利按股权成本贴现的现值。戈登增长模型是其简化版:价值=下期股利÷(股权成本−永续增长率)。它对稳定分红的成熟企业(公用事业)与银行(现金流难定义、以股利为准)尤其适用,但对不分红的成长股几乎无用。

The oldest equity valuation method: a stock is worth the present value of all its future dividends discounted at the cost of equity. The Gordon growth model is its simplified form: value = next dividend ÷ (cost of equity − perpetual growth rate). It suits mature, steady dividend-payers (utilities) and banks (whose cash flows are hard to define, so dividends stand in), but is nearly useless for non-paying growth stocks.

Etymology 词源

dividend（股利）+ discount（折现）——把未来股利折现;DDM 是最早的内在价值模型,先于 DCF。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the Gordon growth model /ðə ˈgɔrdən groʊθ ˈmɑdəl/
    The Gordon growth model values a share as a growing perpetuity of dividends.
  • ▸ dividend-based valuation /ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd-beɪst væljuˈeɪʃən/
    Dividend-based valuation suits the utility and the bank, not the cash-hoarding growth firm.

In the wild 语境例句

For a regulated utility paying a steady, growing dividend, the DDM gives a cleaner value than a DCF wrestling with capex-heavy free cash flow.
