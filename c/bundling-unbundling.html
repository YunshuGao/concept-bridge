打包与拆分 dǎbāo yǔ chāifēnbundling and unbundling

🔊 bundling and unbundling /ˈbəndəlɪŋ ənd ʌnˈbʌndlɪŋ/

打包:把多个产品/服务合并为一个价格出售(有线电视套餐、软件套件),以简化选择、提高客单价、掩盖单品定价、利用范围经济。拆分:把套餐拆成可单独购买的组件,常由挑战者用来只做套餐中利润最丰或体验最差的那一环。

Bundling sells several products or services together at one price (cable packages, software suites) to simplify choice, raise average spend, obscure per-item pricing and exploit scope economies. Unbundling breaks the bundle into separately purchasable pieces — often how a challenger picks off the single most profitable or worst-experienced part of an incumbent's bundle.

Etymology 词源

bundle 源自中古荷兰语 bundel（一捆）——打包即'把多样商品捆成一捆卖'。

  • ▸ unbundling the incumbent /ʌnˈbʌndlɪŋ ðə ɪnˈkʌmbənt/
