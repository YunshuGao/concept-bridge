毛利率 máolìlǜthe gross margin

🔊 gross margin /groʊs ˈmɑrʤən/

（营收−销货成本）÷营收——每一元收入在扣除直接生产/采购成本后剩下多少。高毛利（软件、奢侈品）意味着定价权与规模弹性；低毛利（零售、制造）须靠高周转取胜。是判断商业模式质量的第一指标。

(Revenue − cost of goods sold) ÷ revenue — how much of each sales dollar remains after the direct cost of making or buying the product. High gross margin (software, luxury) signals pricing power and operating leverage; low margin (retail, manufacturing) must win on turnover. A first read on business-model quality.

Etymology 词源

gross 源自拉丁 grossus（粗、大）——毛利即'粗略、未扣除间接费用'的利润，与 net（净）相对。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ gross profit margin /groʊs ˈprɑfɪt ˈmɑrʤən/
    A high gross profit margin buys a company room to spend on growth.
  • ▸ the margin structure /ðə ˈmɑrʤən ˈstrəkʧər/
    The margin structure of a business often reveals its moat before the strategy deck does.
