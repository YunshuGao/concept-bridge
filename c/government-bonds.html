政府债券 zhèngfǔ zhàiquàngovernment bonds

🔊 government bonds /ˈgəvərnmənt bɑndz/

政府发行的借据，承诺按期付息、到期还本。发达国家国债被视为'无风险资产'，其收益率是一切资产定价的基准（无风险利率）。债券价格与利率反向:利率升，存量债券贬值——这就是久期风险。

IOUs issued by governments, promising periodic interest and repayment at maturity. Advanced-economy government bonds are treated as the 'risk-free asset', and their yield is the benchmark for pricing everything else (the risk-free rate). Bond prices move inversely to rates: when rates rise, existing bonds lose value — that is duration risk.

Etymology 词源

bond 源自古英语 bond（束缚、契约）——债券即'一纸还款契约';government bond 亦称 sovereign debt、treasuries、gilts。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ sovereign debt /ˈsɒvrɪn dɛt/
    Sovereign debt is a government's promise, only as good as its willingness and ability to pay.
  • ▸ the risk-free asset /ðə rɪsk-fri ˈæˌsɛt/
    The risk-free asset is the yardstick against which every risky return is measured.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the government bond yield
  • ▸ sovereign debt
  • ▸ treasuries and gilts
  • ▸ the flight to safety

In the wild 语境例句

In a panic, investors dump stocks and buy government bonds — a flight to safety that drives bond prices up and yields down.
