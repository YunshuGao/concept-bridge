🔊 forensic accounting /ˌfɔˈrɛnsɪk əˈkaʊnɪŋ/

用量化模型主动筛查财务造假与破产风险:Beneish M-Score(综合应计、毛利、DSO等八项指标估算操纵概率)、Altman Z-Score(用五个比率预测破产)、Benford定律(异常数字分布)。它们不下定论,但能把值得深挖的可疑对象快速筛出——尽职调查与做空研究的利器。

Using quantitative models to proactively screen for accounting fraud and bankruptcy risk: the Beneish M-Score (combining accruals, gross margin, DSO and five other metrics to estimate the probability of manipulation), the Altman Z-Score (five ratios predicting bankruptcy), and Benford's law (anomalous digit distributions). They don't prove anything, but flag suspects worth digging into — tools of due diligence and short-seller research.

Etymology 词源

forensic 源自拉丁 forensis（属于法庭的,forum 广场/法庭）——鉴证会计即'为法庭般严查'的会计分析。

  • ▸ the Altman Z-Score /ðə ˈɔːltmən zi-skɔr/
    The Altman Z-Score compresses bankruptcy risk into a single, decades-tested number.
