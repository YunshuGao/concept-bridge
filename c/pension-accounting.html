养老金会计 yǎnglǎojīn kuàijìpension accounting

🔊 pension accounting /ˈpɛnʃən əˈkaʊnɪŋ/

设定受益型养老金计划(DB)承诺未来固定给付,企业须估算负债并以资产对冲。'资金缺口'(负债超过计划资产)是隐藏的类债务义务,常藏在附注里。折现率、预期回报率等假设的微调可显著影响利润与负债——是盈余管理与隐性杠杆的温床。

Defined-benefit (DB) pension plans promise fixed future payments, so a firm must estimate the liability and hold assets against it. A funding gap (liabilities exceeding plan assets) is a hidden debt-like obligation, often buried in the footnotes. Tweaks to assumptions — the discount rate, expected return — can materially shift profit and liabilities, making pensions a haven for earnings management and hidden leverage.

Etymology 词源

pension 源自拉丁 pensio（支付,pendere 称量、支付）——养老金即'定期支付的款项';DB=Defined Benefit。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the funded status /ðə ˈfəndɪd ˈstætəs/
    The funded status of a pension is a debt hidden in an actuary's assumptions.
  • ▸ the pension deficit /ðə ˈpɛnʃən ˈdɛfəsət/
    A large pension deficit is a claim on future cash that rarely shows in the headline debt.
