九品中正制 jiǔpǐn zhōngzhèng zhìthe Nine-Rank system

⏳ 220年–隋

曹魏创立的选官制度：在各州郡设中正官，依家世、道德、才能将士人评为九等（上上至下下），朝廷据以授官。初创时兼顾人才，西晋后中正官被世家大族垄断，评定标准唯重家世——“上品无寒门，下品无势族”，成为维护士族特权的工具；至隋被科举制取代。

Cao Wei's recruitment fix: local 'rectifiers' graded gentlemen into nine ranks by pedigree, character and talent, the court appointing accordingly. Capture followed fast — the great families monopolised the rectifierships and pedigree swallowed the other criteria, yielding the era's bitter epigram: 'no poor families in the upper ranks, no powerful clans in the lower'. Aristocracy with a scorecard, until the examinations abolished the scorer.

In the wild 语境例句

'Upper ranks, no poor families' is quoted in every Chinese debate about elite reproduction — the ancient one-liner for what sociologists now measure as intergenerational advantage in admissions and hiring.
