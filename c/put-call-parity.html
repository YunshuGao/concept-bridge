🔊 put-call parity /pʊt-kɔl ˈpɛrəti/

期权定价的基石恒等式:一份看涨期权+行权价的现值现金,等于一份看跌期权+标的资产(同行权价、同到期)。它由无套利原理导出——若关系被打破,即存在无风险套利机会。它把看涨、看跌、标的、无风险债券四者锁在一个方程里,是理解期权与布莱克-斯科尔斯的入口。

A cornerstone no-arbitrage identity of option pricing: a call plus the present value of the strike in cash equals a put plus the underlying asset (same strike, same expiry). It follows from the no-arbitrage principle — if the relationship breaks, a risk-free arbitrage exists. It locks calls, puts, the underlying and a risk-free bond into one equation, and is the gateway to understanding options and Black-Scholes.

Etymology 词源

parity 源自拉丁 paritas（相等,par 同等）——'平价'即两种组合价值相等;违反则套利者立刻'搬平'它。

  • ▸ synthetic positions /sɪnˈθɛtɪk pəˈzɪʃənz/

In the wild 语境例句

If put-call parity is violated, an arbitrageur buys the cheap side and sells the dear side for a risk-free profit — which is exactly why, in liquid markets, it almost always holds.
