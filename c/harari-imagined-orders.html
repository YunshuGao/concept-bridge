想象的秩序（赫拉利三部曲） xiǎngxiàng de zhìxùimagined orders: Harari's trilogy

⏳ 2011年–2024年

《人类简史》（Sapiens）：智人胜出靠认知革命——用虚构故事（imagined orders 想象的秩序：神、国家、货币、公司、人权皆属“互为主体的现实”）实现大规模陌生人协作；农业革命是“史上最大骗局”。《未来简史》（Homo Deus）：人文主义之后的数据主义（dataism）。《智人之上》（Nexus）：信息网络史——信息的功能是连接而非求真，故需自我纠错机制。赫拉利体是当代英语大历史写作的通用语。

Sapiens' engine-idea: Homo sapiens won by the Cognitive Revolution — fiction. Gods, nations, money, corporations and human rights are 'imagined orders', intersubjective realities that let millions of strangers cooperate; the Agricultural Revolution gets billed as 'history's biggest fraud'. Homo Deus extends the arc to dataism after humanism; Nexus recasts it all as the history of information networks, whose job is connection, not truth — hence the need for self-correcting mechanisms. Love it or roll your eyes, Harari-speak is the lingua franca of airport big history, and the Economist reviews each volume like a policy event.
