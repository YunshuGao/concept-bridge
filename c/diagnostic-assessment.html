诊断性评估 zhěnduànxìng pínggūdiagnostic assessment

🔊 diagnostic assessment /ˌdaɪəɡˈnɒstɪk əˈsɛsmənt/

在教学开始前评估学生的已有知识、技能与迷思概念,以便据学情设计教学（'先摸底,再教'）。它是差异化与'从学生所在之处出发'的前提,能暴露先备知识缺口与顽固误解,避免在错误假设上施教。GTPA'了解学生'的关键证据。

Assessing students' prior knowledge, skills and misconceptions before teaching begins, so instruction can be designed from where they actually are ('find out, then teach'). It is the precondition for differentiation and 'starting where the student is', exposing gaps in prior knowledge and stubborn misconceptions before teaching on false assumptions. Key evidence for the GTPA's knowing-students practice.

Etymology 词源

diagnosis 源自希腊 dia（透过）+ gnōsis（认知）——诊断即'透视认知状态';医学隐喻入教学。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ pre-assessment /pri-əˈsɛsmənt/
    Pre-assessment reveals what to skip and what to reteach before you waste a lesson.
  • ▸ surfacing misconceptions /ˈsərfəsɪŋ ˌmɪskənˈsɛpʃənz/
    Surfacing misconceptions early stops you building on a cracked foundation.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ diagnostic pre-test
  • ▸ prior knowledge
  • ▸ surface misconceptions
  • ▸ start where they are

In the wild 语境例句

A short diagnostic before a coding unit reveals half the class already knows loops but none understand variables — reshaping what you teach and to whom.
