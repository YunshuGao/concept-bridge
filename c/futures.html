期货 qīhuòfutures and forwards

🔊 futures and forwards /fˈjuʧərz ənd ˈfɔrwərdz/

约定在未来某日以今天锁定的价格买卖标的资产的合约——期货标准化、交易所交易，远期定制化、场外交易。生产者与用户用它锁定价格、对冲波动;投机者用它下注方向。大宗商品与利率风险管理的核心工具。

Contracts to buy or sell an asset on a future date at a price locked in today — futures are standardised and exchange-traded, forwards are customised and over-the-counter. Producers and users lock in prices and hedge volatility; speculators bet on direction. A core tool for managing commodity and interest-rate risk.

Etymology 词源

future（未来）——在未来交割的合约；forward（向前）同理，二者结构相同而标准化程度不同。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ a forward contract /ə ˈfɔrwərd ˈkɑnˌtrækt/
    A forward contract is a private handshake on tomorrow's price.
  • ▸ locking in the price /ˈlɑkɪŋ ɪn ðə praɪs/
    Locking in the price trades the chance of a windfall for the certainty of a plan.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a futures contract
  • ▸ lock in the price
  • ▸ the spot vs futures price
  • ▸ roll the contract

In the wild 语境例句

A copper miner sells futures to lock in today's price for next year's output — trading potential upside for the certainty its budget needs.
