幕府统治 mùfǔ tǒngzhìthe shogunate

also: the bakufu · shogunal rule

⏳ 1185年–1867年

大化改新百余年后土地兼并盛行，庄园享“不输不入”特权，中央失控；地方豪强武装家族仆从形成武士团。12世纪晚期源赖朝获“征夷大将军”称号、设幕府于镰仓，拥有独立于朝廷的政治军事权力——天皇大权旁落成礼仪摆设，日本进入近700年幕府统治。

As land engrossment hollowed out the Taika state — great estates paying no tax and admitting no official — warrior bands filled the vacuum, and in the late 12th century Minamoto no Yoritomo, named 'barbarian-subduing generalissimo' (shogun), set up his tent government at Kamakura: real power beside a ceremonial emperor, an arrangement that ran Japan for nearly 700 years.

Political scientists love the shogunate as the purest case of the figurehead problem: for seven centuries Japan's emperors reigned while someone else ruled.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

shogun 已入英语词汇（亦作比喻：media shoguns 传媒大亨）。“大权旁落的天皇”即 figurehead（傀儡/礼仪元首）——描述虚位元首的通用词；reign but not rule（统而不治）是现成搭配，后文英国立宪君主也用它。幕府 = bakufu，字面 tent government。
