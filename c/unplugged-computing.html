🔊 unplugged computing /ʌnˈplʌɡd kəmˈpjutɪŋ/

不用电脑、通过实体活动、游戏与人体演示来教计算机科学概念（用扑克牌教排序算法、用寻宝游戏教二分查找、用人体演示网络路由）。它剥离编程语法的干扰,让学生聚焦概念本身,降低认知负荷,也适合设备不足或作为概念引入。计算思维可以先'离屏'建立。

Teaching computer science concepts without computers, through physical activities, games and kinaesthetic demonstrations (card games for sorting algorithms, a treasure hunt for binary search, students acting out network routing). It strips away the distraction of programming syntax, letting students focus on the concept itself, lowers cognitive load, and suits low-resource settings or concept introductions. Computational thinking can be built off-screen first.

Etymology 词源

unplugged（拔掉电源的）——'不插电'即脱离电脑;源自 Bell 等《CS Unplugged》项目。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ CS unplugged /siz ʌnˈplʌɡd/
    CS unplugged teaches sorting with a deck of cards before a line of code.
  • ▸ kinaesthetic computing /ˌkɪnəsˈθɛtɪk kəmˈpjutɪŋ/
    Kinaesthetic computing lets students be the algorithm before they program it.
