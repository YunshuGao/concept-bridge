折旧与摊销 zhéjiù yǔ tānxiāodepreciation and amortisation

🔊 depreciation and amortisation /dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən ənd əˌmɔːrtaɪˈzeɪʃən/

把长期资产的成本在其使用寿命内分期计入费用:有形资产（厂房设备）称折旧，无形资产（专利、软件）称摊销。它是非现金费用（当期不流出现金），却代表真实的资产消耗——这正是 EBITDA 忽略它会误导的原因。

Spreading the cost of a long-lived asset over its useful life as expense: depreciation for tangible assets (plant, equipment), amortisation for intangibles (patents, software). It is a non-cash expense (no cash leaves this period) yet represents real asset consumption — which is why EBITDA, by ignoring it, misleads.

Etymology 词源

depreciate 源自拉丁 depretiare（贬低价值，pretium 价格）——折旧即“价值随使用而递减”；amortise 源自 mort（死）:无形资产“逐年消亡”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ non-cash expense /nɑn-kæʃ ɪkˈspɛns/
    A non-cash expense reduces profit without reducing this period's cash.
  • ▸ capex catching up /ˈkæpɛks ˈkæʧɪŋ əp/
    Depreciation is yesterday's capex quietly billing today's income statement.

In the wild 语境例句

An airline's depreciation is enormous because its planes really do wear out — which is exactly why judging it on EBITDA (before D&A) overstates its earnings power.
