财政政策 cáizhèng zhèngcèfiscal policy

🔊 fiscal policy /ˈfɪskəl ˈpɑləsi/

政府通过调节支出与税收来影响总需求、就业与增长。扩张性财政（增支/减税）刺激经济但扩大赤字；紧缩性财政反之。它与货币政策是宏观调控的两只手，在货币政策失灵（零利率）时尤为关键。

The government's use of spending and taxation to influence aggregate demand, employment and growth. Expansionary fiscal policy (more spending, lower taxes) stimulates but widens deficits; contractionary does the reverse. Alongside monetary policy, it is one of the two hands of macro management — and especially vital when monetary policy is exhausted (at the zero bound).

Etymology 词源

fiscal 源自拉丁 fiscus（钱篮、国库）——财政即'国库的收支管理'。
