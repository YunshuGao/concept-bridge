利特尔法则 lìtè'ěr fǎzéLittle's law

🔊 Little's law /ˈlɪtəlz lɔ/

排队论的基本定律:系统中平均在制品数量=平均到达率×平均停留时间（L=λW）。它简洁地连接了库存、吞吐与周期时间——想缩短交付时间,要么提高吞吐,要么减少在制品。运营与服务设计的通用工具。

A fundamental law of queueing theory: the average number of items in a system = average arrival rate × average time in the system (L = λW). It elegantly links inventory, throughput and cycle time — to shorten lead time, either raise throughput or cut work-in-progress. A universal tool of operations and service design.

Etymology 词源

以数学家 John Little（1961年证明）命名——排队论中最简洁而普适的恒等式。

  • ▸ work-in-progress limits /wərk-ɪn-ˈprɑˌgrɛs ˈlɪmɪts/
    Work-in-progress limits use Little's law to cut cycle time by cutting the queue.
