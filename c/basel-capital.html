巴塞尔资本监管 bāsài'ěr zīběn jiānguǎnBasel capital requirements

🔊 Basel capital requirements /ˈbɑːzəl ˈkæpɪtəl rɪkˈwaɪrmənts/

全球银行监管标准,要求银行按其资产的风险持有最低比例的自有资本作缓冲,以吸收损失、防止倒闭。核心逻辑:资本是抵御亏损的第一道防线,资本越薄、杠杆越高,一点损失就足以让银行资不抵债。2008后不断收紧。

Global banking standards requiring banks to hold a minimum ratio of their own capital against their risk-weighted assets as a buffer to absorb losses and prevent failure. The core logic: capital is the first line of defence against loss, and the thinner the capital (the higher the leverage), the smaller the loss needed to render a bank insolvent. Tightened repeatedly since 2008.

Etymology 词源

以瑞士巴塞尔市（国际清算银行所在地）命名——Basel I/II/III 是全球银行监管的历代协议。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ capital adequacy /ˈkæpɪtəl ˈædəkwəsi/
    Capital adequacy is how much loss a bank can eat before it fails.
  • ▸ the capital buffer /ðə ˈkæpɪtəl ˈbəfər/
    The capital buffer is the shock absorber between a bad year and a bank run.
