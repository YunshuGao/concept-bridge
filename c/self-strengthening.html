洋务运动 Yángwù yùndòngthe Self-Strengthening Movement

🔊 modernisation /ˌmɑdərnəˈzeɪʃən/

⏳ 19世纪60–90年代

内忧外患中，奕䜣、曾国藩、李鸿章、左宗棠、张之洞等洋务派主张学习西方先进技术“自强”“求富”：60年代起办安庆内军械所、江南机器制造总局、福州船政局等军事工业，兴新式学校（京师同文馆）、译西书、派留学生；70年代起办轮船招商局、开平煤矿、汉阳铁厂等民用企业；建新式海陆军（北洋舰队最大、1885设海军衙门）。左宗棠收复新疆（1884新疆建省；1885台湾建省）。中国历史上第一次近代化运动：客观上促进民族资本主义产生、一定程度抵制外资入侵；但以维护清朝统治为根本目的，内有腐败外有挤压，没有使中国走上富强道路（李鸿章自评“纸糊的老虎”）。

After the twin shocks of rebellion and burning palaces, a generation of reforming officials — Li Hongzhang and Zuo Zongtang foremost — tried to buy modernity without changing the state: arsenals and dockyards under 'self-strengthening', then shipping lines, mines and mills under 'seeking wealth'; translation bureaus, new schools, students sent abroad; the Beiyang fleet, and Zuo's reconquest of Xinjiang (made a province in 1884, Taiwan in 1885). It was China's first industrialisation drive — and it failed its own test, as Li admitted in old age: 'paper tigers, all of it... patching and papering over'. Technology grafted onto an unreformed system, the textbook concludes, could not make China strong.

Etymology 词源

modern 源自拉丁 modo（刚刚、现在）→ modernus 当下的——“近代化”即“当下化”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ defensive modernisation /dɪˈfɛnsɪv ˌmɑdərnəˈzeɪʃən/
    Defensive modernisation buys the tools and bars the ideas.
  • ▸ industrialisation drive /ɪnˌdʌstriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən draɪv/
    An industrialisation drive without institutional reform builds paper tigers.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ Chinese learning for essence, Western learning for use
  • ▸ defensive modernisation

In the wild 语境例句

洋务运动 v. 明治维新 is East Asia's great controlled experiment — same decade, same imported hardware, opposite institutional choices — and 1895 graded the papers; every 'why nations fail' course teaches the pair.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“中体西用”（教材未直引但为运动纲领）= Chinese learning as essence, Western learning for use。“纸糊的老虎”即 paper tiger——毛泽东后来使此语全球闻名，其出处在李鸿章的自嘲，绝妙的语词考古。“近代化”= modernisation（中文“近代/现代”两分，英语一词——翻译陷阱）。与 [[meiji-restoration]]、[[peter-the-great]] 构成防御性现代化比较组。
