户籍制度与基层治理 hùjí zhìdù / bǎojiǎhousehold registration and grassroots control

🔊 household registration /ˈhaʊsˌhoʊld ˌrɛʤɪˈstreɪʃən/

⏳ 战国至今

战国始大规模编排民户（秦献公“为户籍相伍”；《商君书》男女皆登记）——户籍是赋役征发的依据，历代极重视：汉“编户齐民”、隋“大索貌阅”、唐三年一造；东晋黄籍白籍与“土断”；宋主户客户；元“诸色户计”按职业定籍世代相袭；明黄册（里甲制为基础，玄武湖册库153万册）；清摊丁入亩后作用削弱、乾隆永停编审。基层组织：秦汉乡里什伍、唐邻保、王安石保甲、明十家牌法、清保甲制（十户一牌十牌一甲十甲一保）——户籍管理与治安监控合一；任务是征发赋役和维护稳定。社会救济：汉常平仓、隋义仓、朱熹社仓，宋以后宗族（范仲淹义田）与慈善组织作用渐大。（西方：中古庄园与城市自治，近代地方自治传统；现代社会保障体系。）

The registered household is China's oldest governing instrument: from Warring-States Qin enrolling every man and woman ('the born recorded, the dead struck off') through Han's 'registered equal subjects', colour-coded refugee registers in the Jin, occupation-castes under the Yuan, to the Ming's Yellow Registers — 1.5 million volumes archived on an island lake — the file that made taxation, conscription and immobility possible. Below it ran mutual surveillance: five-family groups, Wang Anshi's baojia, Qing door-plaques listing every resident, watch-and-report as neighbourhood duty; beside it, the granary line — ever-normal, charity and community granaries — as the state's famine insurance. The chapter is the deep history of two living institutions: the hukou and the grid.

Every migration-and-inequality story in China runs on the word hukou — the 2,300-year-old register that still prices a child's schooling by birthplace; and 'grid management' (网格化) is baojia with a dashboard, as commentators noted during lockdowns.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

户籍 = the household registration (hukou) system——hukou 已入英语；保甲 = the baojia system（同样直接使用）。常平仓 = ever-normal granaries（此译经华莱士影响了美国新政农业政策——制度环流的奇例，链 [[new-deal]]）。“编户齐民”= registered equal subjects。网格化治理 = grid(-based) management——当代延伸词。
