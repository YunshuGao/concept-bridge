隋朝：统一、大运河与速亡 Suícháo / Dàyùnhéthe Sui: reunification, the Grand Canal and swift collapse

🔊 canal /kəˈnæl/

⏳ 581年–618年

581年杨坚代北周建隋（隋文帝），589年灭陈统一全国，结束长期分裂；发展经济编订户籍、统一币制度量衡、加强集权，国力强盛（含嘉仓储粮可供全国五六十年）。605年起隋炀帝征发数百万人开凿以洛阳为中心、北抵涿郡南至余杭、连接五大水系、全长2700多千米的大运河——古代世界最长运河，加强南北政治经济文化交流。但炀帝好大喜功不恤民力：营东都、修长城驰道、三征辽东，民不聊生激起大起义，618年炀帝被杀，盛极一时的隋朝仅37年而亡。

Yang Jian took the northern throne in 581 and the south in 589, ending centuries of division; registers, unified coinage and stocked granaries made Sui formidable fast. Then Emperor Yang spent it all: from 605 millions of conscripts dug the Grand Canal — Luoyang at its hub, Beijing to Hangzhou, 2,700 km linking five river systems, the longest canal of the ancient world and the empire's new north-south artery — while palaces, walls and three Korean wars broke the peasantry. Rebellion answered; Yang was strangled in 618, and the dynasty that stored sixty years of grain lasted thirty-seven years.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析
