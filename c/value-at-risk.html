风险价值（VaR） fēngxiǎn jiàzhívalue at risk (VaR)

🔊 value at risk /ˈvælju æt rɪsk/

用一个数字概括投资组合的风险:在给定置信度（如95%）与时间窗（如一天）内,可能发生的最大损失。它让风险可量化、可比较、可管理,但致命缺陷是对'尾部'（罕见极端事件）估计不足——它告诉你正常日子的坏,却低估灾难日的糟。

Summarising a portfolio's risk in one number: the maximum loss expected over a given horizon (e.g. a day) at a given confidence level (e.g. 95%). It makes risk quantifiable, comparable and manageable — but its fatal flaw is under-estimating the tail: it tells you about a bad normal day and understates the catastrophic one.

Etymology 词源

value at risk（处于风险中的价值）——1990年代 J.P. Morgan 推广,成为银行风险度量的行业标准。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the tail beyond VaR /ðə teɪl bɪɔnd vɑːr/
    The tail beyond VaR is exactly where the crises live.
  • ▸ expected shortfall /ɪkˈspɛktɪd ˈʃɔrtˌfɔl/
    Expected shortfall asks how bad the loss is when VaR is breached, patching one of its holes.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a 95% one-day VaR
  • ▸ breach the VaR limit
  • ▸ tail risk beyond VaR
  • ▸ expected shortfall

In the wild 语境例句

A bank's VaR said it could lose at most 50m on a bad day — then a once-in-a-century event lost it billions, exposing the model's blind spot in the tail.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

VaR 的危险在于制造虚假安全感:它给出一个精确数字,却系统性低估极端事件——'95%的日子安全'恰恰掩盖了那毁灭性的5%。
