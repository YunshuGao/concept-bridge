首次公开发行 shǒucì gōngkāi fāxíngthe initial public offering (IPO)

🔊 initial public offering /ˌɪˈnɪʃəl ˈpəblɪk ˈɔfərɪŋ/

私有公司首次向公众发行股票、在交易所上市——为早期投资人与创始人提供退出与流动性、为公司融资并获得公开货币（股票）用于并购与激励，代价是信息披露、监管与季度业绩压力。

A private company sells shares to the public for the first time and lists on an exchange — giving early investors and founders an exit and liquidity, raising capital, and gaining a public currency (its stock) for acquisitions and incentives, at the cost of disclosure, regulation and quarterly-earnings pressure.

Etymology 词源

initial（首次）+ public（公开）+ offering（发行）——“首次向公众发售股份”；offering 源自拉丁 offerre（呈献）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ going public /goʊɪŋ ˈpəblɪk/
    Going public trades control and privacy for capital and liquidity.
  • ▸ the listing /ðə ˈlɪstɪŋ/
    A listing turns paper wealth into shares anyone can price every second.
