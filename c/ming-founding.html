明朝的建立与皇权强化 Míng / huángquán qiánghuàthe Ming founding and the concentration of imperial power

⏳ 1368年–1644年

元末政治腐败民不聊生，1368年贫苦出身的朱元璋（“高筑墙，广积粮，缓称王”）称帝建明都应天府，攻占大都灭元。为强化皇权全面改制：地方废行中书省设三司（布政、按察、都指挥）；中央废除丞相制度和中书省、六部直接对皇帝负责（立祖制不许复设丞相）、分五军都督府；设皇帝直接指挥的锦衣卫（后有东厂，合称“厂卫”）监视官民；以廷杖羞辱大臣。科举严格限于“四书五经”、以朱熹集注为标准、以“八股文”作答——内容空疏形式呆板脱离实际，禁锢思想，应试者成“皇帝旨意的顺从者”。经济续兴：引进美洲玉米甘薯马铃薯花生，苏州丝织、景德镇青花瓷，晋商徽商商帮出现。

Zhu Yuanzhang — beggar-monk turned rebel strategist ('high walls, full granaries, and don't call yourself king yet') — founded the Ming in 1368 and rebuilt the throne around one man: the chancellorship abolished forever on pain of death to whoever proposed its return, six ministries answering to the emperor alone, provincial power split three ways, the Embroidered Uniform Guard and later the Eastern Depot spying on everyone, and ministers flogged in open court. The examinations narrowed to the Four Books read through Zhu Xi and answered in the ossified 'eight-legged essay' — a machine, the textbook says plainly, for manufacturing obedience. Meanwhile American crops arrived, Jingdezhen fired blue-and-white for the world, and the Shanxi and Huizhou merchant guilds rose.

In the wild 语境例句

The 'eight-legged essay' survives in Chinese as the standard insult for empty formalist writing — 反对八股 was Mao's phrase for bad prose, and every exam-reform debate still invokes it.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

锦衣卫/东厂 = the Embroidered Uniform Guard / the Eastern Depot（明代特务政治 secret police 的原型词）。八股文 = the eight-legged essay。废相是中国皇权史的分水岭——英语表述 the abolition of the chancellorship (1380)，与 [[song-founding]] 分权制衡路线正相反。
