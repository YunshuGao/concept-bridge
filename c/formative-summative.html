形成性评估与终结性评估 xíngchéngxìng yǔ zhōngjiéxìng pínggūformative vs summative assessment

🔊 formative versus summative assessment /ˈfɔːrmətɪv ˈvərsəz ˈsʌmətɪv əˈsɛsmənt/

两类评估的根本区别在于目的:形成性评估在学习过程中进行,用于诊断与调整教学、给出改进反馈（'为了学习'）;终结性评估在学习末尾进行,用于判定成绩与达标程度（'对学习的总结'）。名言:'厨师尝汤是形成性,顾客尝汤是终结性。'两者缺一不可,但日常教学应以形成性为主。

The two types differ fundamentally in purpose: formative assessment happens during learning, to diagnose, adjust teaching and give improvement feedback (for learning); summative assessment happens at the end, to judge grades and attainment (of learning). The saying: 'when the cook tastes the soup it's formative; when the guest tastes it, it's summative.' Both are essential, but day-to-day teaching should lean formative.

Etymology 词源

formative 源自拉丁 formare（塑形）——形成性评估'塑造'尚在进行的学习;summative 源自 summa（总和）——终结性即'总account'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ assessment for vs of learning /əˈsɛsmənt fər ˈvərsəz əv ˈlərnɪŋ/
    Assessment for learning shapes the teaching; assessment of learning grades the result.
  • ▸ tasting the soup /ˈteɪstɪŋ ðə sup/
    Tasting the soup while cooking is formative; serving it is summative.

In the wild 语境例句

A quiz you use to reteach a misunderstood concept is formative; the same quiz counted toward the report grade is summative — purpose, not format, decides.
