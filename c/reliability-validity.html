信度与效度 xìndù yǔ xiàodùreliability and validity

🔊 reliability and validity /riˌlaɪəˈbɪləti ənd vəˈlɪdɪti/

评估质量的两大支柱:效度（validity,评估是否真正测量了它声称要测的东西——用编程题测编程,不是用它测阅读理解）;信度（reliability,评估是否稳定一致——同一作品不同评分者、不同时间打分一致)。二者缺一不可:有信度无效度=精确地测错;有效度无信度=测对但不稳。

The two pillars of assessment quality: validity (does the assessment truly measure what it claims to — testing coding with a coding task, not accidentally testing reading comprehension); reliability (is it stable and consistent — the same work graded the same by different markers and at different times). Both are essential: reliable but invalid = precisely measuring the wrong thing; valid but unreliable = right target, unsteady aim.

Etymology 词源

valid 源自拉丁 validus（强健、有效）——效度即'有效击中目标';reliable 源自 religare（系紧）——信度即'可靠系得住、不飘'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ measuring the right thing well /ˈmɛʒərɪŋ ðə raɪt θɪŋ wɛl/
    Measuring the right thing (validity) well (reliability) is the whole game of good assessment.
  • ▸ construct validity /ˈkɑnstrəkt vəˈlɪdɪti/
    Construct validity asks whether the task really taps the ability you claim to assess.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ valid and reliable
  • ▸ construct validity
  • ▸ consistency of judgement
  • ▸ fair assessment

In the wild 语境例句

A coding test written in dense English may be unreliable-free yet invalid — it partly measures reading, not coding, penalising your EAL/D students unfairly.
