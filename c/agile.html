敏捷开发 mǐnjié kāifāagile

🔊 agile /ˈæʤəl/

以短周期迭代（冲刺 sprint）、跨职能小团队、快速反馈与持续调整取代'一次性大计划'的工作方式。源自软件开发,现扩展至各类知识工作。核心信念:面对不确定,快速试错、边做边学胜过预先完美规划。

A way of working that replaces one-big-plan with short iterative cycles (sprints), small cross-functional teams, fast feedback and continuous adjustment. Born in software, now spread across knowledge work. Its core belief: under uncertainty, rapid trial-and-error and learning by doing beat perfect upfront planning.

Etymology 词源

agile 源自拉丁 agere（做、驱动，agilis 敏捷的）——2001年《敏捷宣言》使其成为软件与管理的运动。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ iterative development /ˈɪtərˌeɪtɪv dɪˈvɛləpmənt/

  • ▸ scrum and sprints /skrʌm ənd sprɪnts/
    Scrum organises the work into sprints so course-correction is never far away.

In the wild 语境例句

Agile teams ship a rough feature in a two-week sprint and learn from real users, rather than spending a year building the wrong thing perfectly.
