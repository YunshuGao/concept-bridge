定位 dìngwèipositioning

🔊 positioning /pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ/

里斯与特劳特:定位不是对产品做什么，而是对潜在顾客的心智做什么——在顾客脑中占据一个独特、简单、可防守的位置（'安全的沃尔沃''最快的联邦快递'）。心智容量有限，第一个占据某认知的品牌最有利。

Ries and Trout: positioning is not what you do to a product but what you do to the mind of the prospect — occupying a distinct, simple, defensible slot in the customer's head ('Volvo is safety', 'FedEx is overnight'). The mind has limited room, and the brand that owns a perception first has the edge.

Etymology 词源

见 competitive-positioning;营销学的 positioning 特指'在顾客心智中的位置',而非战略学的'活动组合'。
