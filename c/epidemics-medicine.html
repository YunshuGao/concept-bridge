疫病、医学与公共卫生 yìbìng yǔ gōnggòng wèishēngepidemics, medicine and public health

🔊 epidemic /ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk/

⏳ 古代至今

疫病深刻影响人类历史：古代天花（康熙曾患）、鼠疫（14世纪黑死病夺走欧洲一半左右人口，劳动力短缺冲击封建生产关系）、1918大流感（死亡2000万以上）等；疫病促成防治努力——中国古代已有隔离（汉“病迁坊”）、明清人痘接种术（后传欧洲）；1796年英国詹纳牛痘接种法；19世纪细菌学（巴斯德、科赫）与1928年弗莱明青霉素（二战广泛应用）革命性进步；中医药宝库（《伤寒杂病论》《本草纲目》针灸；屠呦呦青蒿素）。现代医疗卫生体系：基本医疗卫生体系、医疗服务与保障体系建立完善；新中国爱国卫生运动（“把医疗卫生工作的重点放到农村去”、赤脚医生）、基本消灭血吸虫病天花，人均预期寿命大幅提高——公共卫生为现代国家治理的重要维度。

Disease has been history's silent legislator: the Black Death killing perhaps half of Europe and loosening serfdom's grip, smallpox scarring emperors, the 1918 influenza out-killing the world war. Medicine answered across civilisations — Chinese quarantine wards and variolation (inoculation's ancestor, carried west), Jenner's vaccine (1796), the germ theory of Pasteur and Koch, Fleming's penicillin (1928) mass-produced by war; TCM's pharmacy contributing from the Treatise on Cold Damage to Tu Youyou's artemisinin. The modern state's answer is systemic: universal health infrastructure — in China's case the Patriotic Health Campaigns, barefoot doctors and the great gains in life expectancy that made public health a core measure of governance itself.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析
