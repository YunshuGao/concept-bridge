农奴 nóngnúserfs and serfdom

🔊 serf /sɜːf/

⏳ 9世纪–19世纪

庄园佃户中缺少自由者：劳役与捐税繁重——用领主磨坊面包炉须缴费，女儿外嫁缴“婚姻捐”，子女继承份地缴“继承捐”；生活粗陋贫苦。但劳役按惯例固定，领主不能随意增加，其境遇优于罗马奴隶。

The unfree tenants of the manor, bound to the land and loaded with dues — mill and oven fees, a fine to marry a daughter off the manor, a heavy death duty when children inherited the holding — yet protected by custom: services were fixed, and their lot, though wretched, was a step above the Roman slave's.

Etymology 词源

serf 源自拉丁 servus（奴仆）——农奴一词即罗马奴隶的中世纪转世。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ bondage to the soil /ˈbɒndɪdʒ tɪ ðə sɔɪl/
    Bound to the soil, sold with the soil.
  • ▸ emancipation /ɪˌmænsəˈpeɪʃən/
    Emancipation freed the serf into rent.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ bound to the land
  • ▸ labour dues
  • ▸ emancipation of the serfs
  • ▸ serfdom was abolished

In the wild 语境例句

When Russia freed its serfs in 1861, two years before Lincoln's proclamation, both empires learned that emancipation on paper is the easy half.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

serf（农奴）与 slave（奴隶）在英语中严格区分：农奴附着于土地、受惯例保护、有家庭与份地；奴隶是可买卖的财产。“婚姻捐/继承捐”英语史学称 merchet 与 heriot（冷僻，义译 marriage fine / death duty 即可）。哈耶克书名 The Road to Serfdom（通往奴役之路）使该词进入现代政治词汇。
