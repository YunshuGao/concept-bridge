现金流折现 xiànjīnliú zhéxiàndiscounted cash flow (DCF)

🔊 discounted cash flow /ˈdɪˌskaʊnɪd kæʃ floʊ/

内在价值估值的核心方法:预测企业未来自由现金流，用加权平均资本成本贴现到今天，加上终值的现值，得出企业价值。理论最严谨，但对假设（增长率、贴现率、终值）极度敏感。

The core intrinsic-value method: forecast a firm's future free cash flows, discount them to today at the weighted average cost of capital, add the present value of the terminal value, and you have enterprise value. The most rigorous approach — and acutely sensitive to its assumptions (growth, discount rate, terminal value).

Etymology 词源

discount 源自拉丁 dis-（去除）+ computare（计算）——折现即“算掉时间与风险的价值损耗”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ intrinsic valuation /ˌɪnˈtrɪnsɪk væljuˈeɪʃən/
    Intrinsic valuation prices a business by what it will produce, not what others will pay.
  • ▸ cash flow forecast /kæʃ floʊ ˈfɔrˌkæst/
    A DCF is only as honest as the cash flow forecast beneath it.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ build a DCF model
  • ▸ discount the free cash flows
  • ▸ garbage in, garbage out
  • ▸ terminal value dominates

In the wild 语境例句

A DCF can justify almost any price by nudging the growth and discount rates — which is why analysts triangulate it with market multiples.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

DCF 的致命弱点:终值常占估值的60–80%，而终值又建立在最不确定的远期假设上——“精确的错误”。须做敏感性分析。
