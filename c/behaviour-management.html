课堂行为管理 kètáng xíngwéi guǎnlǐbehaviour management: routines before rapport before rules

🔊 de-escalation /diːˌɛskəˈleɪʃən/

罗杰斯（最少侵入干预、战术性忽略、修复式跟进）、黄（开学头两周立程序）、马扎诺（证据基础）、西格尔（先连接后纠正）——英语教育界的行为管理词汇群:routine、de-escalation、restorative practice、take-up time。

Bill Rogers (least-intrusive intervention, tactical ignoring, repair and rebuild), Wong (the first days establish procedures), Marzano (the evidence base), Siegel (connect before you correct). The working lexicon: routines, de-escalation, restorative practice, take-up time.

Etymology 词源

discipline 源自拉丁 discipulus（学生,discere 学习）——“纪律”与“学科”同词:纪律本义是“使可教”,不是惩罚。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ restorative practice /rɪˈstɔːrətɪv ˈpræktɪs/
    Restorative practice asks who was harmed, not only which rule broke.
  • ▸ tactical ignoring /ˈtæktɪkəl ɪɡˈnɔːrɪŋ/
    Tactical ignoring starves the secondary behaviour while the primary instruction continues.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ least-intrusive intervention
  • ▸ take-up time
  • ▸ the establishment phase
  • ▸ follow up, follow through
  • ▸ connect before you correct

In the wild 语境例句

Give the direction, give take-up time, move away — Rogers in one sentence, and the difference between a request and a standoff.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

discipline 词源即立场:使可学,非使痛苦——与“没有规矩不成方圆”对译时注意英语文献重程序与修复,轻惩戒;NSW 学校话语（PBL/restorative）全在此谱系。
