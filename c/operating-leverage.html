经营杠杆 jīngyíng gànggǎnoperating leverage

🔊 operating leverage /ˈɔpərˌeɪtɪŋ ˈlɛvərɪdʒ/

固定成本占比高的企业，营收增长时利润增长得更快（固定成本被摊薄），但营收下滑时利润跌得也更狠——盈利被放大也被放大风险。与财务杠杆（债务放大）机制类似，但源于成本结构而非资本结构。

A firm with a high share of fixed costs sees profit grow faster than revenue when sales rise (fixed costs spread), but fall harder when sales drop — earnings are amplified both ways. Similar in mechanism to financial leverage (which amplifies via debt), but arising from cost structure rather than capital structure.

Etymology 词源

operating（经营的）+ leverage——固定成本像杠杆一样放大营收变化对利润的影响。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ fixed-cost leverage /fɪkst-kɔst ˈlɛvərɪdʒ/
    Fixed-cost leverage turns a good year into a great one and a soft year into a scare.
  • ▸ margin amplification /ˈmɑrʤən ˌæmpləfəˈkeɪʃən/
    Margin amplification is why software profits explode once fixed costs are covered.

In the wild 语境例句

A software firm with mostly fixed costs shows huge operating leverage: past break-even, each new sale is almost pure profit — but a revenue dip cuts deep.
