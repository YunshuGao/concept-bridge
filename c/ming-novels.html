明代小说与戏剧 Míngdài xiǎoshuōthe Ming novel: Three Kingdoms, Water Margin, Journey to the West

⏳ 元末–明末

明代文学与市民文化结合，小说创作进入黄金时期，长篇章回体小说成熟：罗贯中《三国志通俗演义》（元末明初，以三国史实为基础，反映人民统一愿望，最流行的长篇历史小说之一）；施耐庵《水浒传》（官逼民反、白话传神）；吴承恩《西游记》（明中期浪漫主义神话小说，塑造孙悟空）。戏剧：汤显祖《牡丹亭》批判封建礼教。（书法董其昌、绘画徐渭。）

Print, cities and a paying public made the Ming the golden age of the Chinese novel: Luo Guanzhong's Romance of the Three Kingdoms turned history into East Asia's shared epic of statecraft; Shi Nai'an's Water Margin ('the officials drove the people to rebel') gave outlawry its 108 immortal faces in living vernacular; Wu Cheng'en's Journey to the West sent a monk, a pig and a monkey with attitude across Asia and, in time, into every medium on earth. On stage, Tang Xianzhu's Peony Pavilion — love against ritual propriety — premiered within a decade of Romeo and Juliet.
