🔊 cost-to-serve /kɔst-tɪ-sərv/

精确核算服务每个客户、渠道或订单的全部成本（含物流、退货、支持、折扣），而非只看毛利。常揭示“大客户实则亏损”——收入高但服务成本更高。是客户组合与定价优化的基础。

Precisely accounting for the full cost of serving each customer, channel or order (logistics, returns, support, discounts), not just gross margin. It often reveals that a big customer is actually a loss-maker — high revenue but higher cost-to-serve. The basis for optimising the customer mix and pricing.

Etymology 词源

serve 源自拉丁 servire（服务、侍奉）——cost-to-serve 即“为服务某客户所真正花掉的钱”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ customer profitability /ˈkəstəmər ˌprɑfɪtəˈbɪlɪti/
    Customer profitability, done honestly, reshuffles who your best customers really are.
  • ▸ activity-based costing /ækˈtɪvɪti-beɪst ˈkɔstɪŋ/
    Activity-based costing traces overhead to the activities that actually cause it.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

Cost-to-serve analysis showed the biggest account was the least profitable — its discounts and returns consumed every cent of its margin.
