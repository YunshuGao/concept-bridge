法家 FǎjiāLegalism

🔊 Legalism /ˈligəˌlɪzəm/

⏳ 战国时期

主张君主治国靠法令、权术和威势使臣民慑服；战国末期韩非集大成：反对空谈仁义，强调以法治国、树立君主权威、建立中央集权专制统治。（墨家墨子主张“兼爱”“非攻”、选贤节俭；兵家孙武《孙子兵法》奠定古代军事理论基础。）

Rule by law, technique and awe: men are governed by rewards and punishments, not sermons. Han Fei, the school's synthesiser, scorned talk of benevolence — codify the law, exalt the ruler, centralise all power. (Alongside: Mozi's universal love and anti-aggression, and Sunzi's Art of War, the foundation of Chinese strategy.)

Etymology 词源

legal 源自拉丁 lex/legis（法律）→ legalis；Legalism 大写专指法家。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ realpolitik /ˌrilˌpɔlɪˈtɪk/
    Legalism is realpolitik with a filing system.

In the wild 语境例句

Han Fei reads like Machiavelli with better prose and eighteen centuries' head start — the comparison every intro-to-Chinese-philosophy course makes, because it works.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

致命区分：法家是 rule BY law（法为君主工具）非 rule OF law（法在君上，链 [[twelve-tables]]、[[magna-carta]]）——中西政治传统的分水岭概念。Legalism 首字母大写指法家（小写 legalism 义为“律条主义”）。《孙子兵法》= The Art of War（商学院常销书）；“兼爱”= universal/impartial love。
