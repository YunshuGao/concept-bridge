司马迁与《史记》 Sīmǎ Qiān / ShǐjìSima Qian and the Records of the Grand Historian

⏳ 前1世纪

司马迁继父任太史令，因仗义执言下狱受酷刑，忍辱发愤十余年著成《史记》——中国古代第一部纪传体通史，记传说中黄帝至汉武帝时史事：记帝王将相亦为下层人物立传，公正记事、爱憎分明、秉笔直书，对中国史学影响深远，文学地位亦崇高（“人固有一死，或重于泰山，或轻于鸿毛”）。纪传体为此后“正史”二十四史通用体裁。

Grand historian like his father, Sima Qian spoke up for a disgraced general, suffered castration for it, and chose a decade of shamed labour over honourable death to finish the Shiji: China's first comprehensive history in the biographic mode, from the Yellow Emperor to his own emperor — kings and assassins, ministers and merchants, judged without fear ('a death may be weightier than Mount Tai or lighter than a feather'). All twenty-four dynastic histories after it wear its form.

In the wild 语境例句

Sima Qian is China's Herodotus and Thucydides in one body — the comparison every Western sinology course opens with, usually adding that he out-suffered both.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

《史记》英语定名 Records of the Grand Historian（华兹生译名）。“纪传体”= the annals-biography form；“正史”= the standard/dynastic histories。“秉笔直书”传统 = the historian who writes without fear or favour——与 [[socrates]] 式殉道并为知识人风骨的中西原型。
