流失与留存 liúshī yǔ liúcúnchurn and retention

🔊 churn and retention /tʃɜːrn ənd riˈtɛnʃən/

流失率是某期内流失客户占比,留存率是其反面。对订阅制企业,留存是生死线:高流失如漏水的桶,再多获客也留不住增长。改善留存往往比获取新客更省钱、更能提升终身价值——留存是增长的复利引擎。

Churn is the share of customers lost in a period; retention is its mirror image. For subscription businesses, retention is existential: high churn is a leaky bucket no amount of acquisition can fill. Improving retention is usually cheaper than acquiring new customers and lifts lifetime value more — retention is growth's compounding engine.

Etymology 词源

churn 源自搅拌牛奶的'搅动'——客户'搅动流失';retention 源自拉丁 retinere（保持）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the leaky bucket /ðə ˈliki ˈbəkɪt/
    The leaky bucket is why acquisition without retention is pouring water into sand.
  • ▸ net revenue retention /nɛt ˈrɛvəˌnu riˈtɛnʃən/
    Net revenue retention above 100% means you grow even with the front door shut.

In the wild 语境例句

At 5% monthly churn, a company loses half its customers a year — retention, not acquisition, is the leak that decides whether it grows or drains.
