系统性风险 xìtǒngxìng fēngxiǎnsystemic risk

🔊 systemic risk /sɪˈstɛmɪk rɪsk/

单个机构的倒闭通过相互关联（借贷、衍生品、信心）引发连锁反应、危及整个金融体系乃至实体经济的风险——2008年雷曼倒闭即引爆点。它无法靠分散化消除（因为它同时打击所有资产),是监管与央行'最后贷款人'职能存在的根本理由。

The risk that one institution's failure, through interconnection (lending, derivatives, confidence), triggers a chain reaction endangering the whole financial system and the real economy — Lehman's 2008 collapse being the trigger. It cannot be diversified away (it hits all assets at once) and is the fundamental reason for regulation and the central bank's lender-of-last-resort role.

Etymology 词源

systemic（系统性的）——风险波及'整个系统'而非单个机构;与 systematic（系统性/市场性）近而不同。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ contagion /kənˈteɪʤən/
    Financial contagion spreads panic faster than any fundamental justifies.
  • ▸ interconnectedness /ˌɪntərkəˈnɛktɪdnəs/
    Interconnectedness turns one bank's problem into everyone's crisis.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ financial contagion
  • ▸ too interconnected to fail
  • ▸ the domino effect
  • ▸ macroprudential regulation

In the wild 语境例句

Systemic risk is why one bank's failure can freeze the whole system — interconnection means the panic, not just the loss, propagates.
