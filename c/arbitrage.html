套利 tàolìarbitrage

🔊 arbitrage /ˈɑːrbɪtrɑːʒ/

利用同一资产(或等价现金流)在不同市场或形式间的价格差异,以无风险(或极低风险)方式锁定利润:同时低买高卖。'无套利'是现代资产定价的基本假设——因为套利者会迅速抹平价差。真实世界的套利有限、有风险(见LTCM),但作为定价原理无处不在。

Exploiting a price difference for the same asset (or equivalent cash flows) across markets or forms to lock in a risk-free (or very low-risk) profit: buy low and sell high simultaneously. 'No-arbitrage' is a founding assumption of modern asset pricing — because arbitrageurs quickly erase such gaps. Real-world arbitrage is limited and risky (see LTCM), but as a pricing principle it is everywhere.

Etymology 词源

arbitrage 法语'仲裁、裁定'（拉丁 arbitrari 判断）——套利者'裁定'两地正确价格,使其趋同。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the law of one price /ðə lɔ əv wən praɪs/
    The law of one price says identical assets can't trade at two prices for long.
  • ▸ riskless profit /ˈrɪskləs ˈprɑfɪt/
    A true riskless profit is rare, fleeting, and instantly competed away.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a no-arbitrage condition
  • ▸ the law of one price
  • ▸ limits to arbitrage
  • ▸ risk arbitrage

In the wild 语境例句

If gold is cheaper in London than New York by more than shipping costs, arbitrage buys London and sells New York until the prices converge.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

'无风险套利'在教科书里干净,现实中有限制(资金、时间、模型风险)——LTCM正因'套利趋同'在极端行情下未能兑现而崩溃(见 model-risk)。
