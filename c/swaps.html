🔊 swaps /swɑps/

两方约定在未来一段时间内互相交换现金流的衍生合约:最常见的利率互换(用浮动利率换固定利率)、货币互换。企业用它把浮动利率债务转成固定(对冲利率风险)、或调整货币敞口。名义本金巨大,是场外衍生品市场的主力,也是系统性关联的来源之一。

Derivative contracts in which two parties agree to exchange cash flows over a period: most commonly an interest-rate swap (exchanging a floating rate for a fixed rate) or a currency swap. Firms use them to turn floating-rate debt into fixed (hedging interest-rate risk) or to adjust currency exposure. With huge notional amounts, swaps are the backbone of the OTC derivatives market — and a source of systemic interconnection.

Etymology 词源

swap 源自中古英语 swappen（击掌成交,原指'拍手'）——成交时击掌,引申为'交换'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the interest-rate swap /ðə ˈɪntəˌrɛst-reɪt swɑp/
    An interest-rate swap trades a floating obligation for a fixed one without touching the principal.
  • ▸ a series of forwards /ə ˈsɪriz əv ˈfɔrwərdz/
    A swap can be seen as a series of forward contracts bundled together.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

A company with floating-rate debt fearing rate rises enters a swap to pay fixed and receive floating — converting uncertain future interest into a known cost.
