剩余收益模型 shèngyú shōuyì móxíngthe residual income model

🔊 residual income model /rɪˈzɪʤuəl ˈɪnˌkəm ˈmɑdəl/

从账面价值出发的估值法:股权价值=当前账面净资产+未来各期'剩余收益'(净利润−股权资本成本×账面净资产)的现值。它把经济利润的思想用于股权估值,当股利不稳定、自由现金流难测时尤为有用,与EVA一脉相承。对银行、保险等以账面价值为核心的行业特别贴切。

A valuation method anchored on book value: equity value = current book equity + the present value of future 'residual income' (net income − cost-of-equity capital × book equity). It applies the economic-profit idea to equity valuation, and is especially useful when dividends are erratic and free cash flow is hard to forecast — a cousin of EVA. It fits book-value-centric industries like banks and insurers particularly well.

Etymology 词源

residual（剩余的,拉丁 residuus 留下的）——扣除股权资本成本后'剩余'的收益;与经济利润同源。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the economic-profit approach /ðə ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪk-ˈprɑfɪt əˈproʊʧ/
    The economic-profit approach values the returns a firm earns above its cost of equity.
  • ▸ abnormal earnings /æbˈnɔrməl ˈərnɪŋz/
    Abnormal earnings — profit beyond the capital charge — are what the residual income model capitalises.

In the wild 语境例句

For a bank, where free cash flow is ill-defined, the residual income model values it off book equity plus the returns it earns above its cost of equity.
