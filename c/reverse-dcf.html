🔊 reverse DCF /rɪˈvərs ˌdiːsiːˈɛf/

不用DCF去'算出'目标价,而是反过来问:'当前股价隐含了怎样的增长与利润率假设?'把市场价格倒推为一组增长预期,再判断这些预期是否现实。它把估值从'我认为值多少'转为'市场在押注什么、这个赌注合理吗'——价值投资者识别高估或低估的利器。

Instead of using a DCF to compute a target price, asking the reverse: 'what growth and margin assumptions does the current share price imply?' Backing the market price out into a set of growth expectations, then judging whether they are realistic. It shifts valuation from 'what I think it's worth' to 'what is the market betting, and is that bet reasonable' — a value investor's tool for spotting over- or under-valuation.

Etymology 词源

reverse（反向）——把DCF反过来用:由价格倒推假设,而非由假设推价格。

  • ▸ what's priced in /wəts praɪst ɪn/
    'What's priced in' asks whether the market's implicit forecast can possibly be met.
