激励设计 jīlì shèjìincentive design

🔊 incentive design /ˌɪnˈsɛnɪv dɪˈzaɪn/

芒格箴言:'给我看激励,我就告诉你结果。'人会优化被奖励的东西——常常连带优化了你不想要的副作用。设计激励须防钻空子（度量什么就得到什么）、对齐长期利益、平衡内在与外在动机。组织行为的第一杠杆。

Munger's maxim: 'show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome.' People optimise what is rewarded — often together with side effects you didn't want. Good incentive design guards against gaming (you get what you measure), aligns long-term interests, and balances intrinsic with extrinsic motivation. The first lever of organisational behaviour.

Etymology 词源

incentive 源自拉丁 incentivum（起调、激起，incinere 唱起）——激励即'唤起行动之物'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ incentive alignment /ˌɪnˈsɛnɪv əˈlaɪnmənt/
    Incentive alignment makes doing the right thing also the rewarded thing.
  • ▸ perverse incentives /pərˈvərs ˌɪnˈsɪnɪvz/
    Perverse incentives reward the behaviour that defeats the goal.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ align incentives
  • ▸ perverse incentives
  • ▸ you get what you measure
  • ▸ the cobra effect

In the wild 语境例句

A bounty on dead cobras once bred cobra farms — the classic perverse incentive, and a warning that people optimise the reward, not your intention.
