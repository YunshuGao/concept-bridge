《独立宣言》 Dúlì xuānyánthe Declaration of Independence

🔊 unalienable rights /əˈneɪliˌɛnəbəl raɪts/

⏳ 1776年

1776年7月4日大陆会议通过、杰斐逊起草：宣布人人生而平等，享有生命权、自由权和追求幸福的权利；政府正当权力得自被统治者的同意，人民有权改变或废除损害这些目的的政府。第一个以国家名义表述资产阶级政治要求的纲领性文献，称“第一个人权宣言”——但未废除奴隶制，天赋人权事实上不包括黑人和印第安人。

Jefferson's charter of 1776: all men are created equal, endowed with unalienable rights to life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness; governments derive their just powers from the consent of the governed, and may be altered or abolished when they betray those ends. The first state paper to found a nation on rights — while leaving slavery intact, its self-evident truths withheld in fact from Black and Native Americans.

Etymology 词源

alien（他者）→ alienable（可让渡）→ un-（不可）——“不可让渡的权利”。

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

关键句英文原文值得整句记忆：We hold these truths to be self-evident, that all men are created equal…“天赋人权”对应 natural/unalienable rights（源自洛克 natural rights 传统）；“政府正当权力得自被统治者同意”= the consent of the governed——社会契约论的宪章化。
