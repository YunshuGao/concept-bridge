唐朝的中外文化交流 Táng zhōngwài jiāoliúTang international exchange: kentōshi, Jianzhen, Xuanzang

⏳ 7–9世纪

日本为学习中国先进文化派“遣唐使”十几批（多至500余人），携制度、历法、文字、典籍、建筑技术归国，深刻影响日本社会（端午七夕重阳等节俗亦东传）。扬州高僧鉴真六次东渡（前五次失败、双目失明），754年抵日传佛经与医药文学书法建筑，唐招提寺为其所建、日本国宝。新罗仿唐制、行科举、物产居唐进口首位。贞观初玄奘西行天竺取经，那烂陀寺游学成佛学大师，携大量佛经归长安主持译经；口述《大唐西域记》记百余国山川风物，为中外交流史珍贵文献。唐与大食通好40余次。各国称中国人为“唐人”。

The Tang was everyone's finishing school: Japan sent a dozen kentōshi embassies, five hundred strong, shipping home institutions, calendars, characters and carpentry that rebuilt Japan (and festivals it keeps yet); the blind monk Jianzhen reached Japan on his sixth attempt in 754, founding Tōshōdai-ji, now a national treasure. Silla ran Tang-style exams; Baghdad's caliphate exchanged forty embassies. And Xuanzang walked to India, mastered Buddhist learning at Nalanda, carried the sutras home to a translation bureau in Chang'an — his travelogue of a hundred kingdoms remains Central Asia's best ancient guidebook, and his legend became Journey to the West. Chinatowns are still 'streets of the Tang people'.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析
