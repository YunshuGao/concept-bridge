年中折现惯例 niánzhōng zhéxiàn guànlìthe mid-year convention

🔊 mid-year convention /mɪd-jɪr kənˈvɛnʃən/

DCF建模的技术细节:假设现金流均匀分布于全年、平均在年中而非年末产生,故贴现期采用0.5、1.5、2.5…而非1、2、3。它使估值略微上升(现金流提前半年折现),更贴近现实。看似琐碎,却是分析师专业度的体现——面试官爱问。

A technical detail of DCF modelling: assuming cash flows arrive evenly through the year, on average at mid-year rather than year-end, so discount periods are 0.5, 1.5, 2.5… instead of 1, 2, 3. It nudges the valuation slightly higher (cash discounted half a year sooner) and is more realistic. Seemingly trivial, it signals an analyst's polish — and interviewers love to ask about it.
