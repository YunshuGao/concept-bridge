项飙（猎身、内卷与附近） Xiàng BiāoXiang Biao: body shopping, involution and 'the nearby'

🔊 involution /ˌɪnvəˈluːʃən/

⏳ 2007年/2020年

《全球“猎身”》（Global Body Shopping，普林斯顿出版）：以印度IT劳务体系为对象的民族志——“猎身”指劳务行把程序员作为可全球调配的“身体”囤积转卖，揭示全球化劳动的抽象化机制。项飙其后成为中文公共讨论的关键声音：使“内卷”（involution——格尔茨农业内卷化概念的爆红转义：无发展的竞争加剧）、“悬浮”（suspension——为未来悬置当下）、“附近的消失”（the disappearing nearby）成为时代诊断词。中国学者以英文原创概念再反哺中文公共领域的标本。

Global Body Shopping — a Chinese anthropologist's Princeton-published ethnography of India's IT labour system, where agents warehouse and resell programmers as globally deployable 'bodies' — won the field's top prize and modelled how globalised labour is abstracted. Xiang then became the key public voice of Chinese self-diagnosis: rebooting Geertz's 'involution' as 内卷 (intensifying competition without development — the word of the decade), coining 'suspension' (living deferred for a future) and 'the disappearing nearby'. The loop is the point: a Chinese scholar minting concepts in English that then re-enter Chinese as common speech — your mapping project's ideal case study.
