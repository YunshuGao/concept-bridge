收购价分摊 shōugòujià fēntānpurchase price allocation (PPA)

🔊 purchase price allocation /ˈpərʧəs praɪs ˌæləˈkeɪʃən/

收购完成后,把支付的收购价按公允价值分配到被收购方的各项可辨认资产与负债,超出净资产公允价值的部分记为商誉;可辨认无形资产(客户关系、专利、品牌)被单独确认并摊销,有形资产可能被'评估增值'(step-up)从而增加未来折旧。它直接影响并购后利润与商誉规模。

After a deal closes, allocating the purchase price to the target's identifiable assets and liabilities at fair value, with the excess over net asset fair value booked as goodwill; identifiable intangibles (customer relationships, patents, brands) are recognised separately and amortised, and tangible assets may be 'stepped up' in value, raising future depreciation. It directly shapes post-deal earnings and the size of goodwill.

Etymology 词源

allocation（分配,拉丁 locare 放置）——把收购价'分配'到各项资产;step-up 即资产账面价值'调升'至公允价值。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the asset step-up /ðə ˈæˌsɛt stɛp-əp/
    The asset step-up raises depreciation and thus lowers reported post-deal profit.
  • ▸ goodwill creation /ˈgʊdˈwɪl kriˈeɪʃən/
    Goodwill creation is the plug: whatever the price exceeds identifiable net assets.

In the wild 语境例句

Purchase price allocation stepped up the target's plant and booked large intangibles — the resulting extra depreciation and amortisation dragged pro forma earnings for years.
