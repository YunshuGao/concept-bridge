大化改新 Dàhuà gǎixīnthe Taika Reforms

🔊 reform edict /rɪˈfɔrm ˈidɪkt/

⏳ 646年起

646年起日本仿效唐朝典章制度的改革：政治上建立以天皇为中心的中央集权制，地方设国、郡、里由中央派官治理；经济上废私地私民为公地公民，班田六年一授、不得终身使用或买卖，统一赋税。日本由此成为中央集权制封建国家，大和正式改称日本国。

Japan's remaking of itself on the Tang model from 646: a centralised state under the emperor with centrally appointed provincial officials, private lands and serving-people nationalised into 'public land, public people', fields allotted every six years and unsaleable, taxes standardised — the reform that turned Yamato into 'Nihon'.

Etymology 词源

edict 源自拉丁 edicere（宣告）——诏书即“公开宣告”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ centralisation /ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
    Centralisation copied from Chang'an, adapted at Nara.

In the wild 语境例句

Japan has twice rebuilt itself on a foreign blueprint — Tang China in 646, the West after 1868 — which is why historians call the Meiji Restoration a second Taika.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“天皇”英译固定为 emperor（专指日本时可用 Tennō）；“中央集权封建国家”直译困难——英语表述宜说 a centralised state on the Chinese model，避免 feudal 一词在此的混乱（日本真正的 feudal 阶段恰是后来的幕府时代）。
