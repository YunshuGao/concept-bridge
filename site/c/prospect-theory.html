🔊 prospect theory /ˈprɑspɛkt ˈθɪri/

卡尼曼与特沃斯基的诺奖理论:人不按最终财富的绝对水平、而按相对于参照点的得失来做决策，且对损失比收益敏感、对小概率过度加权。它取代了'理性经济人'，成为行为经济学的理论核心。

Kahneman and Tversky's Nobel-winning theory: people decide not by absolute final wealth but by gains and losses relative to a reference point, weighting losses more than gains and over-weighting small probabilities. It replaced the 'rational economic man' as the theoretical core of behavioural economics.

Etymology 词源

prospect（前景、可能的结果）——人评估的是各种'前景'相对参照点的得失，而非绝对财富。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ reference-dependent choice /ˈrɛfərəns-dɪˈpɛndənt ʧɔɪs/
    Reference-dependent choice means the same outcome delights or dismays depending on the baseline.
  • ▸ probability weighting /ˌprɑbəˈbɪləˌti ˈweɪtɪŋ/
    Probability weighting is why people buy both lottery tickets and insurance.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

Prospect theory explains why the same person buys a lottery ticket and an insurance policy — over-weighting the tiny chances of a jackpot and a disaster alike.
