🔊 accrual accounting /əˈkruəl əˈkaʊnɪŋ/

在收入赚取、费用发生的时点确认，而非现金收付时点——与收付实现制（现金制）相对。它更真实地匹配努力与成果，但依赖估计与判断（何时确认收入、如何摊销），因而也是盈余操纵的入口。

Recognising revenue when earned and expenses when incurred, not when cash changes hands — as opposed to cash accounting. It matches effort and outcome more faithfully but relies on estimates and judgement (when to recognise revenue, how to depreciate), which is also the doorway to earnings manipulation.

Etymology 词源

accrue 源自拉丁 accrescere（增长、累积）——权责发生即“权利与责任一旦发生就入账”，无需等现金。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the matching principle /ðə ˈmæʧɪŋ ˈprɪnsəpəl/
    The matching principle books the cost in the same period as the revenue it created.
  • ▸ revenue recognition /ˈrɛvəˌnu ˌrɛkɪgˈnɪʃən/
    Revenue recognition is where honest judgement and aggressive accounting part ways.
