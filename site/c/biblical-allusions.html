🔊 allusion /əˈluːʒən/

英语的第二语法：好撒玛利亚人（见义勇为者/好人法）、浪子回头（prodigal son）、大卫对歌利亚（以弱胜强）、大马士革路上（damascene conversion,幡然醒悟）、替罪羊（scapegoat）、洗手不干（wash one's hands）、墙上的字（writing on the wall,凶兆）——《经济学人》每期必用。

English's second grammar: the good Samaritan, the prodigal son, David and Goliath, the road to Damascus, the scapegoat, washing one's hands, the writing on the wall. The Economist cannot get through an issue without them.

Etymology 词源

scapegoat 为1530年廷代尔译经自造词:(e)scape + goat——利未记中承载众罪被逐旷野的羊;一次翻译发明了一个概念。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ damascene conversion /ˌdæməˈsiːn kənˈvɜːrʒən/
    The CEO's damascene conversion to green energy coincided with the subsidy.
  • ▸ Goliath /ɡəˈlaɪəθ/
    Every startup pitch needs a Goliath; investors fund the slingshot.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

无需信仰,但不识典故读不懂英文评论——地位相当于汉语的成语典故系统。注意钦定本（KJV）措辞才是习语来源:the powers that be、salt of the earth、go the extra mile 皆 KJV 原文。
