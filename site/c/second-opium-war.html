第二次鸦片战争与火烧圆明园 dì'èrcì Yāpiàn zhànzhēngthe Second Opium War and the burning of the Yuanmingyuan

🔊 plunder /ˈpləndər/

⏳ 1856年–1860年

列强欲进一步打开中国市场，英法借“亚罗号事件”“马神甫事件”发动战争（1856–60）：1858年《天津条约》（公使驻京、增开十口、内河航行），并被迫承认鸦片贸易合法化；1860年英法联军占北京，大肆抢劫后焚毁皇家园林圆明园（雨果：“两个强盗，一个叫法兰西，一个叫英吉利”），签《北京条约》（增开天津、割九龙司、增赔款）。沙俄趁火打劫，19世纪50–80年代通过《瑷珲条约》《北京条约》等割占中国东北西北150多万平方千米领土。半殖民地化程度进一步加深；1861年清设总理衙门办外交。

Wanting more than Nanjing gave, Britain and France manufactured casus belli from a lapsed ship registration and an executed missionary: the war of 1856–60 legalised the opium trade, opened the Yangzi and ten more ports, put ambassadors in Beijing — and, in October 1860, looted and burned the Yuanmingyuan Summer Palace, the act Victor Hugo assigned to 'two robbers, one called France, the other England'. Russia, uninvited, collected the largest prize: 1.5m square kilometres of the northeast and northwest by treaties signed at China's moment of helplessness. The Zongli Yamen — China's first foreign ministry — was founded the next year to manage a world that had forced its way in.
