外部性 wàibùxìngexternalities

🔊 externalities /ˌɛkstɜːrˈnælɪtiz/

一项经济活动对第三方造成的、未被市场价格反映的成本（污染）或收益（教育、疫苗）。负外部性导致过度生产，正外部性导致供给不足——市场失灵的典型。庇古税与补贴、科斯式产权界定是矫正手段。

Costs (pollution) or benefits (education, vaccines) an economic activity imposes on third parties that market prices don't reflect. Negative externalities cause overproduction, positive ones underprovision — a classic market failure. Pigouvian taxes and subsidies, or Coasean property rights, are the corrective tools.

Etymology 词源

external（外部的）源自拉丁 externus（外面的）——外部性即'落在交易双方之外的第三方身上'的成本或收益。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ spillover effect /ˈspɪˌloʊvər ˈifɛkt/
    A spillover effect is the bill for your activity that lands on someone else's desk.
  • ▸ the Pigouvian tax /ðə pɪˈɡuːviən tæks/
    A Pigouvian tax makes the polluter pay the cost it was quietly exporting to everyone.

In the wild 语境例句

Carbon emissions are a negative externality — the polluter's price ignores the cost to the planet, so a carbon tax makes the market tell the truth.
