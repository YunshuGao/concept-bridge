闭关锁国政策 bìguān suǒguóthe closed-door policy and the Canton system

🔊 seclusion /sɪˈkluʒən/

⏳ 18世纪–1840年

清朝统治者以天朝物产丰盈、无需外洋货物自居，又惧沿海居民与外国人交往危及统治，实行严格限制对外贸易的“闭关锁国”政策：顺治时厉行海禁；台湾统一后一度开放四口，后因担忧而收紧；乾隆时仅留广州一处对外通商，由朝廷特许的“广州十三行”统一经营管理对外贸易。闭关锁国面对西方殖民扩张有一定自卫作用，但使中国错失了解世界、学习先进科技与变革的机遇，逐渐落伍于世界发展潮流。

Convinced the Celestial Empire 'possessed all things in abundance' and fearing what coastal subjects might catch from foreigners, the Qing throttled trade to a single valve: from Qianlong's reign, Canton alone, and there only through the thirteen licensed hong merchants who answered for every barbarian ship. The closed door offered a kind of defence against Europe's expanding empires — at the price, the textbook concludes, of missing the world's transformation: the policy that turned the largest economy on earth into the 19th century's prey.

seclusion 源自拉丁 se-（分离）+ claudere（关闭）→ 自我关闭——“闭关”的精确对译。

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“闭关锁国”译 the closed-door policy / seclusion（比较日本 sakoku——链 [[meiji-restoration]]）；制度专名 the Canton system；十三行 = the Thirteen Hongs/cohong。“落后于世界潮流”句式即中国近代叙事的总纲——ch8a（近代史）全册的前提，亦是改革开放话语（opening up）的反义原点。
