科举制 kējǔzhìthe imperial examination system

🔊 examination /ɪgˌzæməˈneɪʃən/

also: the keju system · civil service examinations

⏳ 605年–1905年

魏晋以来选官由上层权贵垄断、看重门第；隋文帝改以考试选才，隋炀帝创进士科标志科举制正式确立。科举加强皇帝选官用人之权、扩大选官范围，使有才学者得以参政、促进社会阶层流动、推动教育发展——中国古代选官制度的一大变革，为历朝沿用约1300年。唐太宗增科目（进士科最重）、武则天创殿试。

Where office had gone by pedigree, the Sui put it up for examination — the jinshi degree under Emperor Yang marking the system's birth. Recruitment by written test centralised patronage in the throne, opened the bureaucracy to talent, set commoners climbing and schools multiplying: the great invention of Chinese government, run for thirteen centuries; Taizong widened the syllabus and Wu Zetian added the palace exam that the emperor graded in person.

Etymology 词源

examination 源自拉丁 examinare（称量，examen 秤舌）——“考试”的本义是把人放上天平。

In the wild 语境例句

When Northcote and Trevelyan designed Britain's civil-service exams in 1854, critics sneered at 'the Chinese principle' — the sneer was accurate: the keju is the direct ancestor of every competitive public-service exam on earth, the gaokao included.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

本知识库的创始示例概念。英译用复数 the imperial examinations 更地道；“进士”= the jinshi (presented scholar) degree；殿试 = the palace examination。mandarin（官话/官员）经葡语入英语，即科举官僚的名字。meritocracy 一词1958年才由 Michael Young 讽刺性造出——描述科举常用 proto-meritocratic。“八股文”后出（见明清），勿提前。
