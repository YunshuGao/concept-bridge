盈余管理 yíngyú guǎnlǐearnings management

🔊 earnings management /ˈərnɪŋz ˈmænɪʤmənt/

管理层在会计准则的弹性范围内(或越界)刻意调节报告利润以达成目标:平滑利润(把好年份的利润藏起来补贴坏年份)、大洗澡(big bath,新任CEO把坏账一次亏光,轻装上阵)、饼干罐储备(cookie-jar reserves,预留准备金待需时释放)。介于合法调节与舞弊之间的灰色地带。

Management deliberately steering reported profit toward a target, within (or beyond) the flexibility of accounting rules: income smoothing (hiding good-year profit to prop up bad years), the big bath (a new CEO writing off everything at once to start clean), and cookie-jar reserves (over-provisioning to release later when needed). The grey zone between legitimate judgement and fraud.

Etymology 词源

manage（管理,源自意大利 maneggiare 驾驭马匹）——盈余'管理'即'驾驭'利润数字走向目标。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ income smoothing /ˈɪnˌkəm sˈmuðɪŋ/
    Income smoothing hides the peaks to fill the troughs, selling a stability that isn't real.
  • ▸ the big bath /ðə bɪg bæθ/
    The big bath kitchen-sinks every loss into one year so the next looks miraculous.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ smooth the earnings
  • ▸ the big bath
  • ▸ cookie-jar reserves
  • ▸ hit the analyst estimate

In the wild 语境例句

A new CEO took a giant 'big bath' write-off in year one — dumping every conceivable loss so the subsequent recovery, and the bonus, looked spectacular.
