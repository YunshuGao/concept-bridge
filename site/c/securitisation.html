证券化 zhèngquànhuàsecuritisation

🔊 securitisation /sɪˌkjʊərɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən/

把一组能产生现金流的资产(房贷、车贷、信用卡应收)打包成资金池,再发行以其现金流为支持的可交易证券(MBS/ABS),并按风险分层(优先级/次级)出售。它盘活流动性、分散风险,但2008年因底层劣质房贷+过度分层+评级失灵而引爆全球危机。

Pooling a set of cash-flow-producing assets (mortgages, car loans, credit-card receivables) and issuing tradable securities backed by those cash flows (MBS/ABS), sold in risk-ranked tranches (senior/junior). It creates liquidity and spreads risk — but in 2008, poor underlying mortgages, excessive tranching and failed ratings turned it into the trigger of a global crisis.

Etymology 词源

securitise = security（证券,拉丁 securus 无忧）+ -ise——把资产'变成证券';2008后成为金融风险的代名词之一。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ asset-backed securities /ˈæˌsɛt-bækt sɪˈkjʊrətiz/
    Asset-backed securities turn illiquid loans into instruments anyone can trade.
  • ▸ tranching /ˈtrɑːnʃɪŋ/
    Tranching slices one pool into safe and risky layers priced separately.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ mortgage-backed securities

  • ▸ pooling and tranching

In the wild 语境例句

Securitisation let banks sell mortgages off their books ('originate to distribute'), which quietly removed the incentive to check whether borrowers could pay — a root of 2008.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

证券化本身中性、有用(分散风险、增流动性);2008的教训是激励扭曲(发放即转售→不审核质量)+过度复杂(CDO²)+评级机构失职,而非工具本身。与 too-big-to-fail、moral-hazard 相连。
