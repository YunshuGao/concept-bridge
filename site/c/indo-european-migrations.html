古代人类迁徙与区域文化 Yìn-Ōu rén qiānxǐthe great migrations: Indo-Europeans and after

🔊 migration /maɪˈgreɪʃən/

⏳ 前2000年至今

约公元前2千纪起，可能起源于东欧平原的印欧人（驯化马、使用马拉战车）大规模迁徙：赫梯人入小亚细亚（冶铁）、波斯人建大帝国、希腊人融合爱琴文明、雅利安人入印度（种姓制度吠陀时代）——深刻影响亚欧大陆区域文化格局。公元3–6世纪亚欧游牧民族大迁徙第二波：匈奴西迁压迫日耳曼人（西罗马灭亡）、五胡内迁（北方民族大交融）——迁徙改变政治版图亦促成文化交融。近代殖民活动引发人口跨地域转移（欧洲人移民美洲大洋洲、非洲奴隶贸易、华工出国——苦力贸易与唐人街），现代移民社会呈多元文化（美国“大熔炉”与文化多样性；新加坡多元和谐）。

History's demography moves in waves. The Indo-Europeans — horse-tamers from the steppe, c. 2000 BC onward — became Hittites in Anatolia, Persians on the plateau, Greeks in the Aegean, Indo-Aryans on the Ganges: one language family's dispersal underlying half the ancient world (why 'mother' sounds alike from Delhi to Dublin). A second wave, 3rd–6th centuries AD, ran the chain Huns → Goths → Rome's fall in the west while the northern peoples merged into China in the east. The modern waves were forced and free together — settlers, slaves and indentured 'coolies' — making today's migration politics, melting pots and Chinatowns this chapter's living epilogue.
