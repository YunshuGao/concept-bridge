创伤心理学（《身体从未忘记》等） chuāngshāng xīnlǐxuétrauma psychology: the body keeps the score

🔊 trauma /ˈtrɔmə/

⏳ 2014年

范德考克《身体从未忘记》：创伤存储于身体与神经系统而非仅记忆——过度警觉、闪回、解离；治疗须经身体（EMDR、瑜伽、正念）而非仅谈话。佩里《登天之梯》：发展性创伤与神经序贯疗法——安抚先于说理（regulate-relate-reason）。沃克《不原谅也没关系》（CPTSD）：复杂性创伤后应激障碍、情绪闪回、内在批判者。ACEs（童年不良经历）研究为公共卫生框架。对教师的直接意义：创伤知情教学（trauma-informed practice）已入学校语汇。

Van der Kolk's thesis made the title a proverb: trauma lives in body and nervous system, not just narrative — hypervigilance, flashbacks, dissociation — so healing must pass through the body (EMDR, yoga, mindfulness), not talk alone. Perry's sequence (regulate, relate, reason — calm before you converse) and Walker's CPTSD map (emotional flashbacks, the inner critic) extend it; the ACEs studies give it public-health teeth. For a teacher the cash value is immediate: 'trauma-informed practice' is now staffroom English, and this shelf is its source code.
