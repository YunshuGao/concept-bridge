关键绩效指标 guānjiàn jìxiào zhǐbiāokey performance indicators (KPIs)

🔊 key performance indicators /ki pərˈfɔrməns ˈɪndəˌkeɪtərz/

衡量组织、团队或流程是否达成目标的量化指标。好的 KPI 少而关键、可操作、难以被操纵。危险在于“度量什么就得到什么”——错误的 KPI 会诱导错误行为。

Quantified measures of whether an organisation, team or process is hitting its goals. Good KPIs are few, actionable and hard to game. The danger: you get what you measure — the wrong KPI drives the wrong behaviour.

Etymology 词源

performance 源自古法语 parfournir（完成、执行）——KPI 即“衡量执行成效的关键指标”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ metrics that matter /ˈmɛtrɪks ðət ˈmætər/
    Metrics that matter change behaviour; vanity metrics only decorate reports.
  • ▸ Goodhart's law /ˈɡʊdhɑːrts lɔ/
    Goodhart's law warns: when a measure becomes a target, it stops measuring well.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ track KPIs
  • ▸ vanity vs actionable metrics
  • ▸ move the metric
  • ▸ what gets measured gets managed

In the wild 语境例句

A call centre rewarded on 'calls per hour' hit the KPI by rushing customers off — a textbook case of Goodhart's law punishing a lazy metric.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

古德哈特定律:一旦指标变成考核目标，它就失去了作为指标的意义（人会优化指标而非目标本身）。设 KPI 须防被“钻空子”。
