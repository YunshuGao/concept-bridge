宋代经济的发展（经济重心南移） Sòngdài jīngjìthe Song economic revolution

⏳ 960年–1279年

两宋农业空前发展：越南占城稻推广、复种两熟制、秧马，水稻跃居粮食首位，“苏湖熟，天下足”；茶棉种植北扩。手工业兴盛：蜀锦江浙丝织、海南棉纺；景德镇成瓷都、江南为制瓷中心；广州泉州明州造船世界领先（水密隔舱、船用指南针、世界现存最早船坞）。商业超前代：开封杭州人口百万，店铺不限地段时段（早市夜市、草市市镇），世界最早商标广告；海外贸易大盛，广州泉州为世界大商港、设市舶司管理，商船远达阿拉伯半岛与非洲东海岸，外贸收入居财政要位。从唐中期开始的经济重心南移至南宋最后完成，中央财政主要来自南方特别是东南。

The Song ran history's first economic revolution: early-ripening Champa rice and double-cropping made the Yangzi delta the granary ('when Suzhou and Huzhou ripen, the empire eats'); Jingdezhen fired its way to becoming the world's porcelain capital; shipwrights launched compass-guided, bulkhead-hulled vessels from the world's oldest surviving dry dock. Commerce burst its ancient cage — shops anywhere, markets at dawn and midnight, the world's first printed trademark — while customs houses (shibosi) at Guangzhou and Quanzhou taxed a seaborne trade reaching Arabia and East Africa that came to anchor state finance. And the centuries-long southward shift of China's economic centre of gravity was complete: the money now came from the south.

In the wild 语境例句

Economic historians' favourite counterfactual lives here: Song China had markets, mills, coke-smelted iron and paper credit around 1100 — 'why not an industrial revolution?' is the China-side of the Great Divergence debate.
