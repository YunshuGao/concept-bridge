社会保障制度（福利国家） shèhuì bǎozhàng zhìdùthe welfare state

🔊 welfare /ˈwɛlˌfɛr/

⏳ 1945年至今

罗斯福新政首建《社会保障法》（养老金、失业保险、救济）；二战后主要资本主义国家为缓和社会矛盾纷纷建立社会保障制度：1948年英国宣布建成“福利国家”，法国联邦德国继之；覆盖医疗、养老、住房、失业保险、教育等，六七十年代进一步发展。是工人阶级斗争的结果，资产阶级亦以之缓和矛盾、稳定环境——但不能解决资本主义制度的基本矛盾。撒切尔：“社会有一个梯子和一张安全网。”

Begun with FDR's Social Security and built out after the war — Britain declared itself a 'welfare state' in 1948, France and West Germany following — the system of health care, pensions, housing, unemployment insurance and schooling was labour's victory and capital's insurance policy in one. Thatcher's formula caught the permanent argument: society needs 'a ladder and a safety net' — and politics is the fight over the size of each.

Etymology 词源

welfare = well + fare（古英语 faran 行走）——“福利”本义“走得好、过得好”。

In the wild 语境例句

Every budget in every rich democracy is a referendum on the same question: how high the ladder, how wide the net, and who pays for the rope.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

英国福利国家蓝图为贝弗里奇报告（the Beveridge Report, 1942），口号 cradle to grave（从摇篮到坟墓）。批评词汇：welfare dependency（福利依赖）、the nanny state（保姆国家）；辩护词汇：social insurance、universal provision。对澳教师实用：Medicare、Centrelink 即此传统的澳洲形态。
