青铜器与礼制 qīngtóngqìritual bronzes

⏳ 前16世纪–前771年

商周青铜器由食器发展为礼器，成为身份地位乃至国家权力的象征，类型数量体现权力大小与森严等级：西周以鼎簋数量区分贵族等级（天子九鼎八簋、诸侯七鼎六簋……）。工匠准确掌握铜锡铅比例，泥范铸造；司母戊鼎（后母戊鼎）重832.84千克，为世界出土最重青铜器；利簋铭文记武王伐纣；三星堆出土金杖、青铜树与人像别具一格。

Shang and Zhou bronze vessels evolved from cookware into the regalia of rank: the number of ding tripods and gui tureens one might use was fixed by grade — nine and eight for the Son of Heaven down the ladder — making tableware a written constitution. Cast in piece-moulds to precise copper-tin recipes, they include the 833kg Simuwu ding, the heaviest bronze ever unearthed, the Li gui whose inscription dates the conquest of Shang, and Sanxingdui's utterly unclassical masks and trees.

In the wild 语境例句

'问鼎' — asking the weight of the king's tripods — still means eyeing the top prize, from the Champions League to the presidency: three thousand years old and in this morning's sports pages.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

鼎 = ding (tripod cauldron)，英语考古文献直接用拼音。“问鼎中原”典出楚庄王——向英语读者解释可作 to covet the throne（字面 ask about the tripods）。“礼器”= ritual vessels；等级用器规定即 sumptuary rules（禁奢令，欧洲中世纪同有——绝佳平行概念）。
