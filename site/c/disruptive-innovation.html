颠覆式创新 diānfù shì chuàngxīndisruptive innovation

🔊 disruptive innovation /dɪsˈrəptɪv ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən/

克里斯坦森:新进入者用更简单、更便宜、起初更差的产品，从低端或新市场切入，随技术改进逐步上攻，最终颠覆只顾服务高端客户的在位巨头。“创新者的窘境”即在位者理性地忽视它。

Christensen's theory: entrants attack from the low end or a new market with simpler, cheaper, initially inferior products, then improve upward until they upend incumbents fixated on high-end customers. The innovator's dilemma is that ignoring it looks rational — until it isn't.

Etymology 词源

disrupt 源自拉丁 dis-（分开）+ rumpere（打破）——颠覆即“打破原有秩序”；该词被硅谷滥用后已近陈词，克氏原义远为精确。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the innovator's dilemma /ðə ˈɪnəˌveɪtər'z dɪˈlɛmə/
    The innovator's dilemma punishes the incumbent for listening to its best customers.
  • ▸ low-end disruption /loʊ-ɛnd dɪsˈrəpʃən/
    Low-end disruption enters where the incumbent is happy to lose margin.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ disrupt from below
  • ▸ the innovator's dilemma
  • ▸ good enough for now
  • ▸ move upmarket

In the wild 语境例句

Digital cameras were 'good enough' and cheap; disruptive innovation let them climb until they dissolved the film business entirely.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

克氏原义严格:并非“任何大变革”，而特指从低端/新市场向上攻的特定路径；媒体把 disruption 泛化为“任何新奇”，是常见误用。
