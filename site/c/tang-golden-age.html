贞观之治与开元盛世 Zhēnguàn zhī zhì / Kāiyuán shèngshìthe Zhenguan and Kaiyuan golden ages

🔊 golden age /ˈgoʊldən eɪʤ/

⏳ 626年–741年

618年李渊建唐都长安；626年李世民即位（唐太宗）。太宗吸取隋亡教训，勤于政事虚心纳谏（魏征进谏200余次，“兼听则明偏信则暗”；太宗以舟水喻君民：“水能载舟亦能覆舟”），广纳贤才（房谋杜断）；完善三省六部制、减省刑罚、增科举科目、考察官吏政绩、轻徭薄赋——“贞观之治”。玄宗前期励精图治，姚崇宋璟辅佐：整顿吏治裁减冗员、发展经济改革税制、注重文教——政治稳定经济繁荣国库充盈，唐朝国力鼎盛，史称“开元盛世”（杜甫：“忆昔开元全盛日，小邑犹藏万家室”）。

The Tang opened with the classic sequel to a unifier's crash: Taizong (r. 626–49) governed by listening — Wei Zheng's two hundred remonstrances, the maxim that 'hearing all sides makes a ruler wise', the famous figure that the people are water and the throne the boat the water can carry or capsize — with a refined three-department, six-ministry state, light taxes and mild law: the Zhenguan model reign, memorised by every later emperor. Two generations on, Xuanzong's early Kaiyuan years (713–41) purged the payroll, reformed taxes and filled the granaries — the Tang at full flood, the benchmark 'golden age' of the Chinese imperial imagination.

Etymology 词源

golden age 译自希腊-拉丁传统（赫西俄德 aurea aetas 黄金时代）——中西同用贵金属喻盛世。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ heyday /ˈheɪˌdeɪ/
    The Kaiyuan years were the dynasty's heyday and its high-water mark.
  • ▸ florescence /flɔːˈrɛsəns/
    Historians call such a florescence rare: peace, plenty and taste at once.

In the wild 语境例句

水能载舟亦能覆舟 is quoted in Chinese politics the way 'consent of the governed' is in American — same intuition, opposite institutional conclusion: a duty on rulers rather than a right of the ruled.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“纳谏”= accepting remonstrance（remonstrance 是描写儒家官僚监督皇权的术语——中华帝国的“忠诚反对”机制，与西方 loyal opposition 对读）。三省六部 = the Three Departments and Six Ministries。“之治/盛世”= a golden age/model reign。魏征之谏 vs 雅典 parrhesia（直言）可作跨文明比较。
