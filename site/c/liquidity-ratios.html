流动性比率 liúdòngxìng bǐlǜliquidity ratios

🔊 liquidity ratios /lɪkˈwɪdɪti ˈreɪʃiˌoʊz/

衡量企业偿付短期债务能力的指标:流动比率(流动资产/流动负债)、速动比率(剔除存货,更严苛)、现金比率(仅现金及等价物)。它们回答'账上的钱够不够还年内到期的债'——流动性危机往往先于盈利危机杀死一家公司。

Measures of a firm's ability to meet short-term obligations: the current ratio (current assets / current liabilities), the quick ratio (excluding inventory, stricter) and the cash ratio (cash and equivalents only). They answer whether there is enough on hand to cover debts due within the year — a liquidity crisis often kills a company before a profitability one does.

Etymology 词源

liquid（流动的,拉丁 liquidus 液态）——资产越易变现越'流动';流动性即'变现的容易程度'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the current ratio /ðə ˈkɑrənt ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ/
    The current ratio asks if a firm could pay its near-term bills without new borrowing.
  • ▸ the quick ratio /ðə kwɪk ˈreɪʃiˌoʊ/
    The quick ratio strips out inventory because it can't always be sold in a hurry.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the current and quick ratios
  • ▸ short-term solvency
  • ▸ a liquidity squeeze
  • ▸ working-capital coverage

In the wild 语境例句

A profitable firm with a current ratio below 1 is a warning: it owes more within a year than it can readily raise — profit on paper, peril in the near term.
