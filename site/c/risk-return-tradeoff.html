🔊 risk-return trade-off /rɪsk-rɪˈtərn treɪd-ɔf/

金融铁律:更高的预期回报只能通过承担更高的风险来获取，没有例外。任何声称'高回报、低风险'的东西，要么是骗局，要么隐藏着尚未显现的风险。它是评估一切投资承诺的第一道过滤器。

Finance's iron law: higher expected returns can only be earned by taking on higher risk, no exceptions. Anything promising 'high return, low risk' is either a fraud or hiding a risk that hasn't shown up yet. It is the first filter for evaluating any investment claim.

Etymology 词源

trade-off（取舍、权衡）——风险与回报之间必须'交换':想要更多回报，就得交出更多安全。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ no free lunch /noʊ fri lənʧ/
    'No free lunch' is the risk-return trade-off stated for cynics.
  • ▸ the risk premium /ðə rɪsk ˈprimiəm/
    The risk premium is the extra return that persuades you to bear the extra risk.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the risk-return trade-off
  • ▸ no risk, no reward
  • ▸ if it's too good to be true
  • ▸ the risk premium

In the wild 语境例句

A scheme promising 15% a year with no risk violates the risk-return trade-off — which is exactly how every Ponzi scheme advertises itself.
