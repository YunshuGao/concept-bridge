分部估值 fēnbù gūzhísum-of-the-parts (SOTP) valuation

🔊 sum-of-the-parts /səm-əv-ðə-pɑrts/

把多元化企业的每个业务分部按最合适的方法单独估值，再加总（减去总部成本与净债务）。常揭示整体市值低于各部分之和——即多元化折价，是分拆与激进投资的定量依据。

Valuing each business segment of a diversified firm separately by its most appropriate method, then adding them up (less head-office costs and net debt). It often reveals the whole trading below the sum of its parts — the conglomerate discount, and the quantitative basis for break-ups and activism.

Etymology 词源

字面“各部分之和”——与整体估值相对；亦称 break-up valuation（拆解估值）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ break-up value /breɪk-əp ˈvælju/
    Break-up value is what the pieces would fetch sold to their best owners.
  • ▸ segment valuation /ˌsɛgˈmɛnt væljuˈeɪʃən/
    Segment valuation refuses to let a hidden gem be priced like its dull siblings.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

An SOTP showed the conglomerate's stake in one listed subsidiary was worth more than the entire group's market cap — the rest came free.
