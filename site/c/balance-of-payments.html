国际收支 guójì shōuzhīthe balance of payments

🔊 balance of payments /ˈbæləns əv ˈpeɪmənts/

记录一国与世界其余所有经济往来的账户:经常账户（贸易、收入）+资本与金融账户（投资、资本流动），两者恒等相抵。经常账户逆差意味着一国消费超过生产，须靠外部资本流入或举债弥补。

The ledger of all a country's economic dealings with the rest of the world: the current account (trade, income) plus the capital and financial account (investment, capital flows), which by identity offset each other. A current-account deficit means a country consumes more than it produces, financed by capital inflows or borrowing.

Etymology 词源

balance（平衡、账目）——国际收支的两大账户必然相抵为零，故为'平衡表'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the current account /ðə ˈkɑrənt əˈkaʊnt/
    The current account tells you whether a nation lives within its means or on the world's credit.
  • ▸ twin deficits /twɪn ˈdɛfɪsɪts/
    Twin deficits — fiscal and current-account together — leave a country doubly dependent on foreign capital.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a current-account deficit
  • ▸ capital inflows
  • ▸ the twin deficits
  • ▸ external financing

In the wild 语境例句

A country running a persistent current-account deficit is borrowing from the world to consume — fine while capital flows in, dangerous when it suddenly stops.
