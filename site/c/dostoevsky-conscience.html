🔊 Dostoevsky /ˌdɒstɔɪˈɛfski/

《罪与罚》（超人理论者拉斯柯尔尼科夫杀人后被良心审判）与《卡拉马佐夫兄弟》（宗教大法官章：人愿以自由换面包）——英语思想写作引用最频的两台俄国思想实验。

Crime and Punishment (Raskolnikov's superman theory broken by conscience) and The Brothers Karamazov (the Grand Inquisitor: men trade freedom for bread) — the two Russian thought experiments English essayists cite most.

Etymology 词源

英译名句 If God does not exist, everything is permitted 实为萨特转述的浓缩——原文更迂回；名句常常是翻译的再创作。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ conscience /ˈkɒnʃəns/
    Conscience is the court that convenes after the acquittal.
  • ▸ the Grand Inquisitor /ðə ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər/
    Every paternalist state rehearses the Grand Inquisitor's defence: bread is kinder than freedom.

  • ▸ the extraordinary man theory
  • ▸ Dostoevskian intensity

In the wild 语境例句

The Grand Inquisitor gets quoted whenever a government offers welfare in exchange for obedience — bread for freedom, the oldest trade.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“自由换面包”与教材中“生存权发展权是首要人权”的论述构成正面对话——同一问题的两种答案，读《经济学人》中国报道时此典频现。
