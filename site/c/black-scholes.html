布莱克-斯科尔斯模型 bùláikè-sīkē'ěrsī móxíngthe Black-Scholes model

🔊 Black-Scholes model /blæk-skoʊlz ˈmɑdəl/

1973年为期权定价的开创性公式——把期权价值表示为标的价格、行权价、时间、无风险利率与波动率的函数。它使衍生品市场得以规模化，是量化金融的奠基石;但其'波动率恒定、价格连续'的假设在危机中失效，酿成风险。

The 1973 breakthrough formula for pricing options — expressing an option's value as a function of the underlying price, strike, time, the risk-free rate and volatility. It let derivatives markets scale and founded quantitative finance; but its assumptions (constant volatility, continuous prices) break down in crises, with dangerous consequences.

Etymology 词源

以 Fischer Black 与 Myron Scholes 命名（Merton 同获诺奖）——把物理学的热扩散方程搬入金融定价。

  • ▸ option pricing theory /ˈɔpʃən ˈpraɪsɪŋ ˈθɪri/

  • ▸ implied volatility /ˌɪmˈplaɪd ˌvɑləˈtɪləti/
    Implied volatility is the market's forecast of turbulence, read backward out of the option price.

Black-Scholes made options tradeable at scale — but its assumption of tame, continuous prices helped blow up a Nobel-laureate hedge fund when markets jumped.
