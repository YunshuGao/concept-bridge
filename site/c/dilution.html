股权稀释 gǔquán xīshìdilution

🔊 dilution /dɪˈluʃən/

发行新股（融资、期权、可转债转股）使现有股东持股比例下降。稀释未必是坏事:若新资本把蛋糕做大，更小的比例可能对应更大的绝对价值。关键看“稀释换来的增长是否值得”。

Issuing new shares (from financing, options or convertibles) reduces existing holders' ownership percentage. Dilution isn't necessarily bad: if the new capital grows the pie, a smaller slice can be worth more in absolute terms. The question is whether the growth bought is worth the ownership given up.

Etymology 词源

dilute 源自拉丁 diluere（冲淡，dis- + luere 洗）——稀释即“把每股的浓度冲淡”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ ownership erosion /ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp ɪˈroʊʒən/
    Ownership erosion is fine if the value of what you own grows faster than your slice shrinks.
  • ▸ anti-dilution protection /ˈænˌti-dɪˈluʃən prəˈtɛkʃən/
    Anti-dilution protection shields early investors when a later round prices lower.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ get diluted
  • ▸ a smaller slice of a bigger pie
  • ▸ anti-dilution provisions
  • ▸ the option pool shuffle

In the wild 语境例句

Taking a big round diluted the founders further, but the capital funded growth that made their smaller stake worth far more than the whole once was.
