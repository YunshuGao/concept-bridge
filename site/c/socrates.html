苏格拉底 SūgélādǐSocrates

🔊 dialectic /ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪk/

⏳ 前469年–前399年

雅典哲学家，将思考转向人类社会，终生探讨灵魂、美德与幸福；主张系统问答求知、“认识你自己”；因“引进新神、腐蚀青年”被雅典法庭判死，拒绝逃亡、饮鸩而死，并以“多数不一定代表正义”质疑简单多数民主制。

The Athenian philosopher who turned inquiry from the cosmos to the human soul, virtue and happiness, teaching through systematic question and answer and the maxim 'know thyself'; condemned by an Athenian jury for impiety and corrupting the young, he refused escape and drank the hemlock — his death a standing challenge to the idea that a majority is the same thing as justice.

Etymology 词源

dialectic 源自希腊 dialegesthai（对话）——辩证法本义“对话术”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ cross-examination /krɔs-ɪgˌzæməˈneɪʃən/
    Cross-examination is the Socratic method with a verdict.

In the wild 语境例句

Law schools still teach by ambush: the Socratic method — relentless questioning until the student's assumptions collapse — is Athens's most durable pedagogy.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

the Socratic method（苏格拉底问答法/产婆术）是英语教育界通用术语。“饮鸩”具体是毒堇汁 hemlock。他对多数决的质疑，英语讨论常表述为 the tyranny of the majority（多数暴政，语出托克维尔/密尔）——雅典处死苏格拉底是这一概念的原型案例。
