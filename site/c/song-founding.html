北宋的政治（重文轻武） Běisòng zhèngzhìthe Northern Song settlement: civil over military

🔊 civilian control /səˈvɪljən kənˈtroʊl/

⏳ 960年–1127年

960年后周禁军统帅赵匡胤陈桥兵变“黄袍加身”建宋（都开封），先南后北结束分裂。深知唐末武将专权之弊，“杯酒释兵权”解除高级将领兵权；控制军队调动使“将不专兵、兵不识将”；分化事权削弱相权；派文臣任州县长官、三年一换，设通判分权、转运使收地方财赋归中央——中央集权强化到前所未有程度。重文轻武：文臣统兵、地位待遇高于武将（“做人莫做军”）；发展科举大增名额、进士优授——营造读书风气、造就文治局面，杜绝兵变政移，但机构重叠冗官冗费、军队战力削弱（岁军费占支出七八成、兵最多120余万）。

In 960 the palace-army commander Zhao Kuangyin was 'robed in yellow' by his troops at Chenqiao and took the throne he was sent to defend; having risen by coup he made coups impossible — dissolving his generals' commands 'over a cup of wine', rotating troops so 'soldiers knew no general and generals owned no army', splitting the chancellorship, staffing every prefecture with three-year civilian appointees, and piping local taxes straight to the capital. The Song chose brush over sword: civil officials outranked and even commanded the military, examination quotas exploded and a scholar-official state was born — coup-proof and culturally brilliant, but bloated with redundant offices and chronically weak at war, its enormous army eating four-fifths of the budget yet losing to every neighbour.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“重文轻武”= privileging the civil over the military；“士大夫”= the scholar-official class/literati（宋型政治的主角，英语汉学核心词）。“黄袍加身”“杯酒释兵权”可意译：robed in yellow by his troops；dissolving commands over a cup of wine。coup-proofing 是现代比较政治学术语——本课即其古典案例。
