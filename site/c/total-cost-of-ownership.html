总拥有成本 zǒng yōngyǒu chéngběntotal cost of ownership (TCO)

🔊 total cost of ownership /ˈtoʊtəl kɔst əv ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp/

一项资产或采购在其全生命周期内的真实总成本,而非仅购买价——包括安装、运营、维护、能耗、培训、停机与处置。便宜的采购价常掩盖高昂的持有成本('买得起,用不起')。B2B 采购与资本决策的核心分析框架。

The true total cost of an asset or purchase over its whole life, not just the sticker price — including installation, operation, maintenance, energy, training, downtime and disposal. A cheap purchase price often hides a costly ownership burden ('cheap to buy, dear to run'). A core framework in B2B procurement and capital decisions.

Etymology 词源

total（总）+ cost of ownership（拥有成本）——不止购买价,而是'从买到弃'的全部成本;缩写 TCO 是采购与 IT 决策常用词。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ lifecycle cost /ˈlaɪfsaɪkəl kɔst/
    Lifecycle cost sees the whole bill; the purchase price is only the deposit.
  • ▸ the iceberg cost /ðə ˈaɪsbərg kɔst/
    The iceberg cost is what sits below the waterline of the sticker price.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the total cost of ownership
  • ▸ cheap to buy, dear to run
  • ▸ lifecycle cost
  • ▸ hidden ownership costs

In the wild 语境例句

The cheaper machine had a higher total cost of ownership — its energy use and downtime over ten years dwarfed the price gap the buyer first fixated on.
