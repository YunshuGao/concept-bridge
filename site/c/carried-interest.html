附带权益 fùdài quányìcarried interest (carry)

🔊 carried interest /ˈkɛrid ˈɪntəˌrɛst/

私募/风投普通合伙人（GP）从基金利润中分得的份额——通常在返还本金并达到门槛收益后取20%。它是 GP 的核心激励，也是“2/20”收费结构（2%管理费+20%业绩分成）中的“20”，并因税率优惠而长期具政治争议。

The share of a fund's profits the general partner keeps — typically 20% after returning capital and clearing a hurdle. It is the GP's core incentive, the '20' in the '2 and 20' fee structure (2% management fee, 20% of profits), and perennially controversial for its favourable tax treatment.

Etymology 词源

源自中世纪地中海航运:船长'carry'货物获利后分成——GP 'carries' 投资人的资本，分享增值，故名 carry。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the carry /ðə ˈkæri/
    The carry is where fund managers make their fortunes, not the management fee.
  • ▸ performance fee /pərˈfɔrməns fi/
    A performance fee aligns the manager with the investor — up to the point it doesn't.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ 2 and 20
  • ▸ the hurdle rate
  • ▸ the carry pool
  • ▸ the tax treatment of carry

In the wild 语境例句

On a fund that doubles, carried interest hands the GPs 20% of the gains — the reason the '20' matters far more to them than the '2'.
