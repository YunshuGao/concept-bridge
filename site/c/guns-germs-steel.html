《枪炮、病菌与钢铁》（地理假说） qiāngpào bìngjùn gāngtiěGuns, Germs, and Steel: the geography hypothesis

🔊 determinism /dɪˈtərməˌnɪzəm/

⏳ 1997年

戴蒙德对“亚力的问题”（为何是欧洲人征服美洲而非相反）的回答：各大洲命运差异源于地理禀赋而非人种——欧亚大陆可驯化动植物多、东西轴线利于传播、人畜共处孕育病菌免疫（天花灭美洲——枪炮病菌钢铁三件套中病菌最致命）。反种族主义的环境决定论，亦因“决定论”倾向被批评（对比制度学派《国家为什么失败》）。

Diamond's answer to Yali's question — why did Europeans conquer the Americas and not the reverse? Geography, not genes: Eurasia drew the domesticable plants and animals, its east-west axis spread them, and millennia beside livestock brewed the germs (smallpox above all) that did most of the conquering. An anti-racist environmental determinism — and the standing target of the institutions school (Why Nations Fail), making Diamond v. Acemoglu the default 'why the West' seminar debate.
