维特根斯坦（语言的界限） WéitègēnsītǎnWittgenstein: the limits of language

🔊 language game /ˈlæŋgwɪʤ geɪm/

⏳ 1921年/1953年

蒙克传记《天才之为责任》呈现的双期维特根斯坦：前期《逻辑哲学论》——“我的语言的界限意味着我的世界的界限”“对于不可说的，必须保持沉默”（语言为世界画界）；后期《哲学研究》自我推翻——意义即使用（meaning is use），语言是嵌入生活形式的语言游戏（language games），家族相似取代本质定义；“哲学问题的形式是：我找不着北”。对双语者的直接意义：每学会一种新说法，即挪动一次世界的界限——本知识库的哲学守护神。

Monk's Duty of Genius gives both Wittgensteins: the early Tractatus — 'the limits of my language mean the limits of my world'; 'whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent' — and the later Investigations that demolished it: meaning is use, language is a family of games embedded in forms of life, and philosophy's problems have the form 'I don't know my way about'. For a bilingual learner the cash value is immediate: every new way of saying is a border moved — which makes him, quietly, this knowledge base's patron philosopher.
