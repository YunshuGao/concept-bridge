衍生品 yǎnshēngpǐnderivatives

🔊 derivatives /dərˈɪvətɪvz/

价值'派生'于标的资产（股票、债券、商品、汇率、利率）的金融合约:期权、期货、远期、互换。用于对冲风险、价格发现或投机。它们能有效转移风险，也因高杠杆与不透明成为系统性危机的放大器（2008）。

Financial contracts whose value derives from an underlying asset (stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, rates): options, futures, forwards, swaps. Used to hedge, discover prices or speculate. They transfer risk efficiently — and, through leverage and opacity, can amplify systemic crises (2008).

Etymology 词源

derive 源自拉丁 derivare（引水、导出，rivus 溪流）——衍生品的价值'导流自'标的资产。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ contingent claims /kənˈtɪnʤənt kleɪmz/
    Contingent claims pay off only if the specified future arrives.
  • ▸ financial weapons of mass destruction /ˌfaɪˈnænʃəl ˈwɛpənz əv mæs dɪˈstrəkʃən/
    Buffett called derivatives 'financial weapons of mass destruction' for their hidden, leveraged linkages.
