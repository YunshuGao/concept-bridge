递延所得税 dìyán suǒdéshuìdeferred tax assets and liabilities

🔊 deferred taxes /dɪˈfərd ˈtæksɪz/

会计利润与税务利润因确认时点不同而产生的暂时性差异,形成递延税项资产(未来可抵税,如可结转亏损)或递延税负(未来须补税,如加速折旧)。分析师需判断递延税资产能否实现(估值备抵),以及大额递延税负对未来现金税负的影响。

Temporary differences between accounting profit and taxable profit, arising from timing, create deferred tax assets (future deductions, e.g. loss carryforwards) or deferred tax liabilities (future taxes owed, e.g. accelerated depreciation). Analysts judge whether a deferred tax asset is realisable (the valuation allowance) and how a large deferred tax liability will hit future cash taxes.

Etymology 词源

defer 源自拉丁 differre（推迟）——递延即'推迟到未来期间'确认的税项差异。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the deferred tax asset /ðə dɪˈfərd tæks ˈæˌsɛt/
    A deferred tax asset is only worth something if the company will earn enough profit to use it.
  • ▸ the valuation allowance /ðə væljuˈeɪʃən əˈlaʊəns/
    A valuation allowance is management admitting a deferred tax asset may never be realised.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ deferred tax asset/liability
  • ▸ the valuation allowance
  • ▸ loss carryforwards
  • ▸ cash vs book taxes

In the wild 语境例句

A loss-making startup's huge deferred tax asset is worthless without future profits — the valuation allowance reveals whether management believes those profits will come.
