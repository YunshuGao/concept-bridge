福尔摩斯与推理 fúěrmósīSherlock Holmes & deduction

🔊 deduction /dɪˈdʌkʃən/

柯南·道尔塑造的咨询侦探：英语中理性推断的人格化身。名言“排除一切不可能，剩下的再不可思议也是真相”；elementary、no-brainer 式的 Sherlock 反讽（“可真是大侦探啊”）皆入日常英语。

Conan Doyle's consulting detective personifies inference in English. 'When you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains must be the truth'; calling someone 'Sherlock' is now standard sarcasm for stating the obvious.

Etymology 词源

严格说福尔摩斯多用溯因推理（abduction，皮尔士术语：由果推最佳解释）而非演绎（deduction）——小说把术语用“错”了一百多年，逻辑课的经典开场白。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ abduction /æbˈdʌkʃən/
    Abduction guesses the hypothesis that would make the clues unsurprising.
  • ▸ no smoking gun /noʊ ˈsmoʊkɪŋ ɡʌn/
    Without a smoking gun, the case rests on the dog that did not bark.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ no shit, Sherlock
  • ▸ the dog that didn't bark
  • ▸ eliminate the impossible
  • ▸ a Watson to his Holmes

In the wild 语境例句

The auditors worked like Holmes: the fraud showed not in what the ledger said, but in the entries that did not bark.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

the dog that didn't bark（沉默的狗＝缺失的证据本身是证据）是《经济学人》高频典故。名句 Elementary, my dear Watson 原著从未出现——典故考据的又一案例。
