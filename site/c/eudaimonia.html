幸福（eudaimonia） xìngfúeudaimonia: flourishing

🔊 eudaimonia /ˌjuːdaɪˈmoʊniə/

希腊伦理学核心:eudaimonia 非快乐情绪,而是“人的繁盛”——把人之为人的功能(理性、德性)发挥到极致的一生。雅典扩张文化的精神内核,亚里士多德《尼各马可伦理学》的最高目的。

The keystone of Greek ethics: eudaimonia is not a pleasant mood but human flourishing — a whole life spent exercising our defining functions (reason, virtue) at their best. The spirit of expansive Athens; the highest end in Aristotle's Ethics.

Etymology 词源

eudaimonia = eu(好)+ daimōn(守护神)——本义“有好的守护神眷顾”;英译 happiness 太轻,学界多保留希腊原词或译 flourishing。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ flourishing /ˈflʌrɪʃɪŋ/
    Flourishing is happiness with a verb tense — something you do across a lifetime.
  • ▸ the good life /ðə ɡʊd laɪf/
    The good life, for the Greeks, was a performance, not a feeling.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ human flourishing
  • ▸ virtue ethics
  • ▸ living well and doing well
  • ▸ the highest good (summum bonum)

In the wild 语境例句

Positive psychology rebranded eudaimonia as 'flourishing' — a 2,400-year-old idea with a new citation index.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

翻译陷阱:eudaimonia≠happiness(情绪),≈flourishing(整全的人生成就)——中文“幸福”亦偏情绪,故此概念中英皆需注解。与斯多亚 equanimity、儒家“修身”并列可比。
