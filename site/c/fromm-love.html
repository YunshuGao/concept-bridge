弗洛姆《爱的艺术》 Fúluòmǔ / ài de yìshùFromm: The Art of Loving

🔊 alienation /ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən/

⏳ 1956年

弗洛姆三部曲（《爱的艺术》《论不服从》《存在的艺术》）核心：爱不是坠入的运气而是需要学习的能力/艺术——包含关心、责任、尊重、了解四要素；成熟的爱是“在保全自身完整性前提下的结合”（我因为爱你所以需要你，而非因为需要你所以爱你）；现代资本主义把爱异化为交换与消费（“人格市场”）。占有（to have）还是存在（to be）的生存方式之问贯穿三书——法兰克福学派的人本主义一翼。

The trilogy's heart: love is not a happening but a faculty — an art demanding knowledge and effort, composed of care, responsibility, respect and knowledge; mature love is 'union under the condition of preserving one's integrity' ('I need you because I love you', not the reverse); and consumer capitalism corrupts it into exchange on a 'personality market'. Behind it, the To Have or To Be question that names Fromm's whole humanism — the Frankfurt School's warmest export.

Etymology 词源

alienation 源自拉丁 alienus（他者的）——异化即“变成他者之物”，马克思-弗洛姆共用词。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the art of loving /ðə ɑrt əv ˈləvɪŋ/
    Loving is an art: studied, practised, failed at, resumed.
  • ▸ having and being /ˈhævɪŋ ənd biɪŋ/
    Having hoards; being unfolds.

  • ▸ the art of loving

In the wild 语境例句

'Love is a verb' — the couples-counselling cliché — is Fromm dissolved into pop culture; 允许爱情消失 (also on this shelf) is the same conversation continued in Chinese.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

《逃避自由》Escape from Freedom 为其名著背景（自由的重负——链 [[courage-disliked]] 被讨厌的勇气=自由的代价，两书同题异答）。异化 = alienation（马克思概念的心理学化——链 [[marxism]]）。占有/存在 = having/being modes。
