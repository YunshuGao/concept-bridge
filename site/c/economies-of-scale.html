规模经济 guīmó jīngjìeconomies of scale

🔊 economies of scale /ɪˈkɑnəmiz əv skeɪl/

产量越大，单位成本越低——固定成本被摊薄、议价力增强、专业化分工深化。规模经济是成本领先与进入壁垒的根本引擎，但过度扩张会遇到规模不经济。

As output rises, unit cost falls — fixed costs spread, bargaining power grows, specialisation deepens. Scale economies are the engine of cost leadership and entry barriers, though past a point diseconomies of scale set in.

Etymology 词源

economy 源自希腊 oikonomia（家政、oikos 家+nomos 治理）——“经济”本义治家；规模经济即“把家做大反而更省”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ unit cost curve /ˈjunɪt kɔst kərv/
    The unit cost curve slopes down until complexity bends it back up.
  • ▸ minimum efficient scale /ˈmɪnəməm ɪˈfɪʃənt skeɪl/
    Below minimum efficient scale, you are subsidising your larger rivals.

In the wild 语境例句

A chip fab costs the same to run at 60% or 95% utilisation — which is why scale economies force the industry toward a few giants.
