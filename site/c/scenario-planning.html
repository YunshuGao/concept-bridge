🔊 scenario planning /sɪˈnɛrioʊ ˈplænɪŋ/

面对深度不确定性，不去预测单一未来，而是构建几种内部自洽、差异显著的未来情景，检验战略在各情景下的稳健性。壳牌1970年代用它预判石油危机而闻名。

Under deep uncertainty, rather than forecasting one future, build several internally consistent, sharply different scenarios and stress-test strategy against each. Shell's use of it to anticipate the 1970s oil shocks made the technique famous.

Etymology 词源

scenario 源自意大利 scenario（舞台布景说明，scena 舞台）——情景即“可能上演的剧本”，规划即预排多套剧本。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ what-if analysis /wət-ɪf æˈnælɪsɪs/
    What-if analysis replaces a single fragile forecast with a robust range.
  • ▸ robustness testing /roʊˈbəstnəs ˈtɛstɪŋ/
    Robustness testing asks not what will happen but what would still work.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

Instead of forecasting one oil price, Shell modelled several worlds — so when the shock came, it had already rehearsed the response.
