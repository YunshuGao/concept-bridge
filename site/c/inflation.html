通货膨胀 tōnghuò péngzhànginflation

🔊 inflation /ˌɪnˈfleɪʃən/

一般物价水平的持续上升，即货币购买力的下降。温和通胀是经济润滑剂，恶性通胀摧毁货币信用与储蓄。它由需求拉动、成本推动或货币超发引起，是央行货币政策的首要目标（通常锚定2%）。

A sustained rise in the general price level — a fall in the purchasing power of money. Mild inflation lubricates the economy; hyperinflation destroys monetary trust and savings. Driven by demand-pull, cost-push or excess money, it is the primary target of central-bank policy (usually anchored around 2%).

Etymology 词源

inflate 源自拉丁 inflare（吹胀，in + flare 吹）——通胀即'物价被吹胀、货币被稀释'。
