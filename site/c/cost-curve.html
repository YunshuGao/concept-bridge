成本曲线 chéngběn qūxiànthe industry cost curve

🔊 cost curve /kɔst kərv/

把行业内所有产能按单位成本从低到高排列，横轴累计产量、纵轴成本——市场价由边际（最后一个被需要的）产能决定。低成本产能赚取“租金”，高成本产能在周期低谷出局。大宗商品分析的核心工具。

Ranking all industry capacity from lowest to highest unit cost — cumulative volume on the x-axis, cost on the y — the market price is set by the marginal (last needed) producer. Low-cost capacity earns rent; high-cost capacity exits in downturns. A core tool of commodity analysis.

Etymology 词源

curve 源自拉丁 curvus（弯曲）——成本曲线即把产能成本画成一条上行的阶梯线，直观呈现谁在赚钱、谁在赔钱。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the marginal producer /ðə ˈmɑrʤənəl prəˈdusər/
    The marginal producer sets the price and makes almost nothing doing it.
  • ▸ cost position /kɔst pəˈzɪʃən/
    Your cost position on the curve decides whether a downturn is a threat or an opportunity.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ move down the cost curve
  • ▸ the marginal cost of supply
  • ▸ first-quartile cost position
  • ▸ the price-setter

In the wild 语境例句

In iron ore, being first-quartile on the cost curve means you profit at prices that bankrupt the miners above you.
