一次性项目与利润正常化 yícìxìng xiàngmùone-time items and normalisation

🔊 one-time items /wən-taɪm ˈaɪtəmz/

重组费用、资产减值、诉讼赔偿、出售收益等非经常性项目会扭曲当期利润。分析师须把它们剔除,还原'正常化'的可持续盈利能力,才能估值与比较。陷阱:管理层倾向把好事算经常、把坏事算一次性('每年都有的一次性费用')——须警惕'惯常的非经常项'。

Restructuring charges, asset impairments, litigation settlements and gains on disposals are non-recurring items that distort reported profit. Analysts strip them out to recover 'normalised', sustainable earning power for valuation and comparison. The trap: management tends to call good things recurring and bad things one-off ('one-time charges' that appear every year) — beware the recurring non-recurring item.

Etymology 词源

normalise 源自拉丁 norma（木匠角尺、标准）——正常化即'把利润调回标准、可持续的水平'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ normalised earnings /ˈnɔrməˌlaɪzd ˈərnɪŋz/
    Normalised earnings strip out the noise to show what the business really earns in a typical year.
  • ▸ non-recurring items /nɑn-rɪˈkərɪŋ ˈaɪtəmz/
    Non-recurring items that recur every year are neither non-recurring nor honest.

In the wild 语境例句

An analyst normalising earnings noticed 'one-time' restructuring charges in five consecutive years — recurring costs dressed up as exceptional to flatter adjusted profit.
