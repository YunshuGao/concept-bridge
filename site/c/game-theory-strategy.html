博弈论战略 bóyìlùn zhànlüègame theory in strategy

🔊 game theory /geɪm ˈθɪri/

把竞争当作参与者相互预判、相互反应的博弈来分析:纳什均衡、囚徒困境、可信承诺、信号与威慑。核心提醒:你的最优选择取决于对手会如何回应——不能只做单方最优化。

Analysing competition as a game where players anticipate and react to each other: Nash equilibrium, the prisoner's dilemma, credible commitment, signalling and deterrence. The core reminder: your best move depends on how rivals will respond — you cannot optimise one-sidedly.

Etymology 词源

由 von Neumann 与 Morgenstern（1944）奠基——把战略互动数学化；Nash 均衡使其成为经济学与商业的通用语。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ strategic interaction /strəˈtiʤɪk ˌɪnərˈækʃən/
    Strategic interaction means your payoff has someone else's decision inside it.
  • ▸ credible commitment /ˈkrɛdəbəl kəˈmɪtmənt/
    A credible commitment changes rivals' behaviour precisely because you can't back out.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the Nash equilibrium
  • ▸ a prisoner's dilemma
  • ▸ credible threats and commitments
  • ▸ anticipate the reaction

In the wild 语境例句

Two rivals stuck in a price-war prisoner's dilemma both lose — game theory shows why a credible commitment to hold price can rescue both.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

商业博弈多为“非零和”:合作可扩大蛋糕（正和），并非只有你死我活。误把一切当零和是新手常见错误。
