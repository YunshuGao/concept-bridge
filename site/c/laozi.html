老子与道家 Lǎozǐ / DàojiāLaozi and Daoism

🔊 Daoism /ˈdaʊɪzəm/

⏳ 春秋后期

春秋后期楚国人，道家学派创始人，曾任周朝史官。认为万物运行有其自然法则、人应顺应自然；事物皆有对立面且可相互转化（难易、长短、祸福）；政治上主张“无为而治”。学说集中于《老子》（《道德经》）。战国庄子发展其学，强调顺应自然民心、追求精神自由与独立人格。

A Zhou archivist from Chu and the fountainhead of Daoism: all things follow a natural way (dao) that force cannot improve; opposites — hard and easy, fortune and ruin — turn into one another; the wisest government 'acts without acting' (wuwei). His slim Daodejing became the school's classic; Zhuangzi later stretched the teaching toward inner freedom and the integrity of the self.

Etymology 词源

Dao 为“道”音译（威妥玛作 Tao）；-ism 源自希腊 -ismos，构成学说名——Daoism 即“道”之学。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ quietism /ˈkwaɪətɪzəm/
    Daoist quietism counsels ruling a state like frying a small fish.
  • ▸ non-action /nɑn-ˈækʃən/
    Non-action is not inaction but acting without forcing.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the Dao/the Way
  • ▸ wuwei (effortless action)
  • ▸ go with the flow
  • ▸ the Daodejing
  • ▸ yin and yang

In the wild 语境例句

Silicon Valley rediscovers wuwei every decade under new names — 'don't fight the market', 'ship and iterate' — management theory's laissez-faire has a Chinese elder brother.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

规范拼写：Daoism/Dao（学术）与 Taoism/Tao（传统威妥玛，仍常见）并存。“无为”译 non-action 易误解为消极——effortless action / acting by not forcing 更达意。wuwei 与 [[adam-smith]] 的 laissez-faire 常被并论（“治大国若烹小鲜”即最早的最小干预论）。
