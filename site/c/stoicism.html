斯多亚主义（《沉思录》与爱比克泰德） Sīduōyà zhǔyìStoicism: Marcus Aurelius and Epictetus

🔊 equanimity /ˌɛkwəˈnɪmɪti/

⏳ 1–2世纪

书库中《沉思录》（皇帝的私人札记）、爱比克泰德（奴隶出身的哲人）《论说集》及Stoic Six Pack：核心是控制二分法（dichotomy of control——有些事取决于我们，有些不；只为前者负责）、依自然而活、消极想象（premeditatio malorum 预想失去以珍惜当下）、把障碍变道路（“行动之障碍推进行动”）。当代复兴为硅谷与体育界的操作系统（Ryan Holiday）；与儒家修身、道家顺应的比较是中西心性论的常设议题。

The emperor's private notebook (Meditations) and the freed slave's lectures (Epictetus) teach one operating system: the dichotomy of control — some things are up to us, some are not, and serenity lives in the difference; live according to nature; rehearse loss to relish presence (premeditatio malorum); 'the impediment to action advances action'. Reborn as Silicon Valley's and locker-room philosophy, and the standing western partner in any comparison with Confucian self-cultivation ([[zeng-guofan]]) or Daoist acceptance ([[laozi]]).

Etymology 词源

equanimity 源自拉丁 aequus（平）+ animus（心）——“平心”即斯多亚之的。

    Self-mastery begins at the border of what is yours to command.

In the wild 语境例句

Small-s 'stoic' in everyday English ('she was stoic at the funeral') is this school fossilised into an adjective — the same lexicalisation路径 as [[sparta]]'s spartan.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

控制二分法 = the dichotomy of control（爱比克泰德《手册》开篇）。amor fati（爱命运）、memento mori（勿忘死）以拉丁语直接流通。马可·奥勒留 = Marcus Aurelius（[[roman-empire]] 的哲学家皇帝——五贤帝之一，历史与哲学在此合流）。
