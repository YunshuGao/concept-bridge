华盛顿 HuáshèngdùnGeorge Washington

🔊 precedent /ˈprɛsɪdənt/

⏳ 1732年–1799年

弗吉尼亚种植园主之子，早年任军队指挥官；独立战争中团结各州、坚持对英作战，赢得独立；1787年主持制宪会议；1789年当选美国第一任总统，首都以其名命名。

Virginia planter's son and wartime commander who held the states together to victory, chaired the Constitutional Convention, and became the first president — then, as memorably, gave the office up.

Etymology 词源

precedent 见前——华盛顿的辞职即最伟大的“先例”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ farewell address /ˌfɛrˈwɛl ˈæˌdrɛs/
    The farewell address warned against the parties it predated.

In the wild 语境例句

Washington's greatest act was an abdication: by walking away after two terms he set the precedent that the office outranks the man — the norm every strongman since has tested.
