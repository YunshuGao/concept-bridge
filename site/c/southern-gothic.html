🔊 grotesque /ɡroʊˈtɛsk/

美国南方文学流派:衰败庄园、畸零人（grotesques）、暴烈的恩典。奥康纳《好人难寻》:平庸之恶遇上枪口下的顿悟;麦卡勒斯《伤心咖啡馆之歌》:爱者与被爱者的不对等定律。

Decaying gentry, grotesques, violent grace. O'Connor's A Good Man Is Hard to Find puts banal selfishness before a gun-barrel epiphany; McCullers' Ballad of the Sad Café states the law that the lover and the beloved come from different countries.

Etymology 词源

grotesque 源自意大利 grottesca（洞窟壁画风格,grotta 洞穴）——“怪诞”的词根是出土罗马洞窟里的奇异壁画。
