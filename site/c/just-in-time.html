准时制 zhǔnshízhìjust-in-time (JIT)

🔊 just-in-time /ʤɪst-ɪn-taɪm/

精益生产的核心:只在需要的时刻、按需要的数量生产或采购,把库存降到最低。它释放被库存占用的资金、暴露流程问题、逼迫质量,但以低冗余换效率,对供应中断极为敏感——效率与韧性的取舍。

The core of lean production: produce or procure only what is needed, when needed, in the amount needed, driving inventory to a minimum. It frees up cash tied in stock, exposes process problems and forces quality — but trades slack for efficiency, leaving it acutely vulnerable to supply disruption. Efficiency versus resilience.

Etymology 词源

just in time（恰好及时）——零部件'恰好在需要时'到达生产线,而非提前堆积成库存。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ lean inventory /lin ˌɪnvənˈtɔri/
    Lean inventory is cash freed from the warehouse and put back to work.
  • ▸ pull production /pʊl pərˈdəkʃən/
    Pull production makes demand summon supply, not the other way round.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ just-in-time inventory
  • ▸ zero inventory
  • ▸ the pull system
  • ▸ just-in-case buffers

In the wild 语境例句

Just-in-time let manufacturers run on almost no inventory — a triumph of efficiency until a single chip shortage idled entire car plants.
