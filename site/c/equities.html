股票 gǔpiàoequities (stocks)

🔊 equities /ˈɛkwətiz/

代表企业所有权份额的证券——股东分享利润（分红）与增值，但承担最末位、最高的风险。长期看，股票回报高于债券与现金（股权风险溢价），是财富增值的核心引擎，代价是短期高波动。

Securities representing ownership stakes in companies — shareholders share in profits (dividends) and appreciation, but bear the last, riskiest claim. Over the long run, equities out-return bonds and cash (the equity risk premium), the core engine of wealth-building, at the price of short-term volatility.

Etymology 词源

见 cost-of-equity;equity 源自拉丁 aequitas（公平）——股票即'公平分享企业剩余'的凭证。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ shares /ʃɛrz/
    Shares turn a company into something millions can own a sliver of.
  • ▸ ownership capital /ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp ˈkæpɪtəl/
    Ownership capital is patient money that rides the whole journey, up and down.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ the equity risk premium
  • ▸ stocks for the long run
  • ▸ dividends and capital gains
  • ▸ equity volatility

In the wild 语境例句

Over decades equities have beaten bonds and cash by a wide margin — the reward, per the risk-return trade-off, for enduring their stomach-churning swings.
