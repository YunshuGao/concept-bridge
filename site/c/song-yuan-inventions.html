活字印刷、指南针与火药 huózì yìnshuā / zhǐnánzhēn / huǒyàomovable type, the compass and gunpowder

⏳ 宋元时期

隋唐发明雕版印刷；北宋毕昇发明活字印刷术（胶泥刻字烧硬、铁板排版，“印数十百千本则极为神速”——沈括《梦溪笔谈》记载），后有木活字、王祯转轮排字、元代铜活字；13世纪传入朝鲜，后经丝路传波斯、经蒙古西征等途径入欧。战国司南、宋代人造磁铁罗盘，北宋末海船始用指南针（“阴晦观指南针”为世界航海用罗盘最早记录），经阿拉伯商人传入欧洲，大大促进世界远洋航海。唐代发明火药、唐末用于军事，宋元火器广用于战争（突火枪为枪炮始祖、元代火铳），13世纪传入阿拉伯、14世纪初经阿拉伯人传欧——对欧洲火器制造与作战方式产生巨大影响，推动欧洲社会变革。培根：印刷术、火药、磁石“改变了整个世界的面貌和状态”。

Three Song-era technologies rewired the world. Bi Sheng's movable type (c. 1040s, recorded by the scientist Shen Kuo: 'for hundreds or thousands of copies, marvellously quick') spread via Korea and the steppe to remake the book. The magnetic compass, aboard Chinese ships by the 1100s — the world's first recorded navigational use — reached Europe through Arab middlemen and opened the oceans. And gunpowder, a Tang discovery weaponised under the Song (the bamboo 'fire-lance' fathering every gun), crossed to the Arabs by the 13th century and Europe by the 14th, where it blew up the castle and the knight. Francis Bacon, not knowing all three were Chinese, judged that no empire or sect had 'exerted greater power over human affairs'.
