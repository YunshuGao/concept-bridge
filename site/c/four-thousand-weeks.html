《四千周》 sìqiān zhōuFour Thousand Weeks: time and finitude

🔊 finitude /ˈfɪnɪtjuːd/

伯克曼反生产力之书:人生约四千周,时间管理的真问题不是装下更多,而是接受装不下——有限性（finitude）。效率是陷阱:清空收件箱只会招来更多邮件;真正的选择是“战略性亏欠”。

Burkeman's anti-productivity book: a life is about four thousand weeks, and the real task is not fitting more in but accepting that most things won't fit — finitude. Efficiency is a trap; the empty inbox refills. Choose what to neglect, strategically.

Etymology 词源

finitude 源自拉丁 finis（边界、终点）——define、finish、finance 同根:定义、完成、清账,都是“划界”。
