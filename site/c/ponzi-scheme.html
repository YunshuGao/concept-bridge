🔊 Ponzi scheme /ˈpɒnzi skim/

用后来投资者的本金冒充'投资收益'支付给早期投资者,制造高额稳定回报的假象,本质是拆东墙补西墙。它必然崩溃——一旦新资金流入放缓就穿帮。识别它的第一问永远是:回报从何而来?'稳定的高回报'是最响的警报。

Paying early investors with the capital of later ones, disguised as investment returns, to fake high steady profits — in essence robbing Peter to pay Paul. It must collapse — the moment new money slows, the fraud is exposed. The first question to detect it is always: where do the returns actually come from? 'Steady high returns' is the loudest alarm.

Etymology 词源

以1920年代骗子 Charles Ponzi 命名——他用邮政票据骗局完善了这一模式;Madoff 是史上最大案例。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ a pyramid scheme /ə ˈpɪrəmɪd skim/
    A pyramid scheme needs ever more recruits below to pay those above.
  • ▸ robbing Peter to pay Paul /ˈrɑbɪŋ ˈpitər tɪ peɪ pɔl/
    Robbing Peter to pay Paul works only until Peter stops showing up.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ a Ponzi scheme
  • ▸ unsustainable returns
  • ▸ where do the returns come from?
  • ▸ too good to be true

In the wild 语境例句

Madoff's steady, unvarying returns — impossible in real markets — were the tell: a Ponzi scheme paying old investors with new money until the inflows dried up.
