🔊 irony /ˈaɪrəni/

⏳ 1964年

霍恩1964年名著：“澳大利亚是个幸运的国家，由二流人物经营，全凭好运分享他人的想法”——书名本是讽刺（靠资源与殖民遗产而非创新致富），却被国民欢快误用为自夸，作者抱憾终身。与《Girt Nation》（戏谑体澳洲史）同为理解澳洲自我叙事的钥匙：cultural cringe（文化自卑）、tall poppy syndrome（高罂粟综合征——枪打出头鸟）、fair go（公平机会）、mateship——NSW 生活与教师职场的语用背景。

Horne's 1964 barb — 'Australia is a lucky country run mainly by second-rate people who share its luck' — was written as indictment (rich by minerals and inheritance, not ideas) and adopted by the nation as a compliment, to the author's lifelong exasperation: the most successfully misread title in Australian letters. With Girt Nation's irreverent history it unlocks the local self-talk your staffroom runs on — cultural cringe, tall poppy syndrome, fair go, mateship — the idiom set that no migrant professional in NSW can skip.
