现实主义文学（巴尔扎克与托尔斯泰） xiànshízhǔyì wénxuéthe realist novel: Balzac and Tolstoy

🔊 realism /ˈriəlɪzm/

⏳ 19世纪

巴尔扎克以“人间喜剧”系列（《欧也妮·葛朗台》《高老头》）细绘转型期法国社会万象，留下巴黎上流社会变迁史——《高老头》写尽“一切为了金钱”的社会中家庭亦成欺骗掠夺之场。稍后俄国托尔斯泰以《战争与和平》《安娜·卡列尼娜》《复活》描绘各阶层生活，抨击沙皇专制与地主压榨、又否定暴力革命宣扬博爱，被列宁称为“俄国革命的镜子”。

Balzac's Human Comedy anatomised money-mad France in transition — in Old Goriot even the family is a market — while Tolstoy's War and Peace, Anna Karenina and Resurrection painted every stratum of Russia, lashing autocracy and landlordism yet preaching love over revolution: 'the mirror of the Russian revolution', in Lenin's phrase.

Etymology 词源

realism 源自拉丁 res（事物）——现实主义即“事物主义”。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ social novel /ˈsoʊʃəl ˈnɑvəl/
    The social novel audits a nation by household.
  • ▸ panorama /ˌpænərˈæmə/
    A panorama in prose: money, marriage, ruin.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ literary realism

In the wild 语境例句

Critics call sprawling condition-of-the-nation novels 'Balzacian' and moral epics 'Tolstoyan' — the two adjectives divide the realist inheritance between them.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“现实主义”= (literary) realism；“人间喜剧”= The Human Comedy。《安娜·卡列尼娜》开篇句是英语最常引用的小说首句之一：All happy families are alike…——经济学借为 the Anna Karenina principle（成功须万事俱备，失败一因足矣）。
