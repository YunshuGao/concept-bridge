🔊 learning intentions and success criteria /ˈlərnɪŋ ˌɪnˈtɛnʧənz ənd səkˈsɛs kraɪˈtɪəriə/

向学生明示'今天要学什么'（learning intention,亦称WALT——We Are Learning To）与'达标长什么样'（success criteria,亦称WILF——What I'm Looking For）。它让学习目标透明可见,使学生能自我监控进度、自评作品。是可见学习与形成性评估的日常抓手,澳洲课堂的标配。

Making explicit to students what they are learning today (the learning intention, also WALT — We Are Learning To) and what success looks like (the success criteria, also WILF — What I'm Looking For). It makes the goal transparent, letting students monitor their own progress and self-assess their work. A daily tool of visible learning and formative assessment, standard in Australian classrooms.

Etymology 词源

intention 源自拉丁 intendere（伸向、瞄准）——学习'意图'即课堂瞄准的目标;WALT/WILF 为课堂化的记忆缩写。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ WALT and WILF /wɔːlt ənd wɪlf/
    WALT and WILF put the lesson's target and its bar in language students can hold.
  • ▸ making learning visible /ˈmeɪkɪŋ ˈlərnɪŋ ˈvɪzəbəl/
    Making learning visible lets students judge their own work against the criteria.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ share the learning intention
  • ▸ co-construct success criteria
  • ▸ WALT and WILF
  • ▸ against the success criteria

In the wild 语境例句

Posting the success criteria — 'a working loop, a comment on each block, tested with two inputs' — lets students check their own code before you ever mark it.
