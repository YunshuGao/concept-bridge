武士与武士道 wǔshì yǔ wǔshìdàothe samurai and bushido

🔊 bushido /ˈbuːʃɪdəʊ/

⏳ 10世纪起

地方豪强为保护庄园，把家族与仆从中的青壮男子武装成血缘与主从制结合的军事集团，成员称“武士”；武士集团长期统治逐渐形成武士道——武士效忠的对象不是国家，而是他的主公。

Warrior bands raised by provincial magnates to guard their estates, bound by blood and lord-vassal ties; centuries of warrior rule bred bushido, the samurai code — whose loyalty ran not to the state but to one's own lord.

Etymology 词源

bushidō 日语“武士道”直借——bushi（武士）+ dō（道）。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ warrior code /ˈwɔrjər koʊd/
    A warrior code aestheticises obedience.
  • ▸ retainer /rɪˈteɪnər/
    The retainer's loyalty ran to his lord, not his land.

Corporate Japan long traded on samurai imagery — salarymen as retainers, the firm as their lord — an ethic of loyalty to the house rather than the market.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

samurai、bushido 均已入英语。注意教材点睛处：效忠对象是主公而非国家——英语表述 loyalty was personal, not national，这与欧洲封臣制（my vassal's vassal…）形成绝佳对照，也解释了何为 feudal Japan（日本才是贴切用 feudal 的亚洲案例）。
