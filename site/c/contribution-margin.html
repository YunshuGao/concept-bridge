边际贡献 biānjì gòngxiànthe contribution margin

🔊 contribution margin /ˌkɑntrəˈbjuʃən ˈmɑrʤən/

单位售价减去单位变动成本——每卖一件产品为覆盖固定成本、创造利润所“贡献”的金额。它是定价、盈亏平衡、产品取舍与规模决策的核心:只要边际贡献为正，多卖一件就摊薄一分固定成本。

Unit price minus unit variable cost — what each sale 'contributes' toward covering fixed costs and creating profit. It is the core of pricing, break-even, product mix and scaling decisions: as long as the contribution margin is positive, each extra unit dilutes fixed costs further.

Etymology 词源

contribution（贡献）——每单位销售'贡献'给固定成本与利润的部分；margin 源自拉丁 margo（边缘）:利润的'边际'。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ marginal profit /ˈmɑrʤənəl ˈprɑfɪt/
    Marginal profit on the next unit is what really decides whether to make it.
  • ▸ variable-cost margin /ˈvɛriəbəl-kɔst ˈmɑrʤən/
    The variable-cost margin shows how much each sale helps once the fixed costs are set.

Collocations 常用搭配

In the wild 语境例句

A software firm's contribution margin is near 100% — each extra copy costs almost nothing to make, so every sale drops straight toward profit.
