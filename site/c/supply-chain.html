供应链 gōngyìngliànthe supply chain

🔊 supply chain /səˈplaɪ ʧeɪn/

从原材料到成品交付给终端客户所经历的全部组织、活动、信息与资源的网络。管理它是在成本、速度、可靠性与韧性之间求平衡。全球化把供应链拉得又长又薄,效率极高却极脆弱——'即时'与'以防万一'的世纪之争。

The whole network of organisations, activities, information and resources that carries a product from raw material to the end customer. Managing it means balancing cost, speed, reliability and resilience. Globalisation stretched supply chains long and thin — hyper-efficient yet fragile — reigniting the century's debate between 'just-in-time' and 'just-in-case'.

Etymology 词源

supply（供应)+ chain（链）——从源头到终端环环相扣的'供应之链';每一环的中断都会沿链传导。

Synonyms 近义词

  • ▸ the value network /ðə ˈvælju ˈnɛtˌwərk/
    The value network is the supply chain seen as a web, not a line.
  • ▸ just-in-case resilience /ʤɪst-ɪn-keɪs rɪˈzɪljəns/
    Just-in-case resilience buys buffers that just-in-time efficiency sold off.

Collocations 常用搭配

  • ▸ supply chain resilience
  • ▸ reshoring and nearshoring
  • ▸ supply chain disruption
  • ▸ end-to-end visibility

In the wild 语境例句

The pandemic taught firms that a supply chain optimised only for cost is a liability — pushing a shift from just-in-time toward just-in-case buffers.
