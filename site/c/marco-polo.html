元代驿站与马可·波罗 Mǎkě Bōluóthe Yuan post-roads and Marco Polo

🔊 travelogue /ˈtrævəˌlɔg/

⏳ 1271年–1295年

元朝疆域辽阔，修覆盖全国的陆路交通网、四通八达的驿站（“急递铺”日夜兼程一昼夜400里），陆路西通中亚西亚东欧；海上丝绸之路进入鼎盛（宋代通商五六十国，元代140多国）。中国印刷术火药指南针纸币驿站制度西传，西方药物天文历法数学东来。意大利旅行家马可·波罗经丝路来华、在元生活17年，归国后口述《马可·波罗行纪》记大都杭州泉州等城市风物，反映中国富庶先进，激起欧洲人对东方的极大向往。（郭守敬《授时历》测年365.2425天，早现行公历约300年。）

The Yuan wired Eurasia: post-stations blanketing the empire (express riders covering 400 li a day), caravan roads running to the Black Sea, and a maritime silk route at its peak — 140 trading partners against the Song's sixty. Down these channels went printing, gunpowder, the compass, paper money and the postal idea itself; back came Western astronomy, medicine and mathematics (Guo Shoujing meanwhile fixing the year at 365.2425 days, three centuries before the Gregorian calendar matched it). And along them came a Venetian who served Kublai seventeen years: Marco Polo, whose Travels — dictated in a Genoese jail — introduced Europe to cities it could not imagine and desires it could not shake.
