交子（世界最早纸币） jiāozǐjiaozi: the world's first paper money

🔊 banknote /ˈbæŋkˌnoʊt/

⏳ 北宋前期

商贸繁荣使货币交易量激增，金属货币携带不便；北宋前期四川地区出现“交子”——世界上最早的纸币；南宋时纸币（会子）发展成与铜钱并行的货币。

Song commerce outgrew its coinage — iron cash by the cartload — and Sichuan's merchants invented the escape: jiaozi, printed promises to pay, soon a state monopoly and by the Southern Song (as huizi) legal tender alongside bronze coin. Paper money had entered the world seven centuries before Europe dared it.

Etymology 词源

banknote 由 bank（意大利 banca 长凳——钱商柜台）+ note（拉丁 nota 记号）复合——票据即“柜台记号”。

    All fiat money is a promise wearing a seal.

In the wild 语境例句

Every central-bank explainer on fiat money starts in Chengdu: the jiaozi is exhibit one, and Yuan-era hyperinflation exhibit two — the whole modern monetary debate in miniature, seven hundred years early.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

“纸币”= paper money/banknotes；“法定货币”= legal tender；“信用货币”= fiat money（fiat 拉丁语“让它成为”）。马可·波罗对元钞的惊叹是欧洲首次听闻纸币——链 [[marco-polo]]。元末滥发致通胀亦为 hyperinflation 案例库成员（链 [[versailles]] 的魏玛）。
