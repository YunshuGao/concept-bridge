李翊云（用第二语言写作） Lǐ YìyúnYiyun Li: writing in an adopted language

⏳ 2005年至今

书库中最完整的作家专柜：北京长大、北大生物系、赴美读免疫学博士后转写作，只用英语写作并拒绝作品中译——“私人的语言”论（中文承载太多不欲回访的记忆，英语是选择而非继承的语言）。《千年敬祈》《漂泊者》（小说）、《亲爱的朋友》（谈抑郁与阅读的散文）、《理由终结之处》（与自杀离世之子的虚构对话）、Things in Nature Merely Grow（丧子续章）。以第二语言达到文体巅峰（麦克阿瑟天才奖）——对以英语重建概念世界的学习者，是最深的同行者文本。

The library's most complete single-author shelf: Beijing-raised, Peking University biology, an immunology PhD track abandoned for fiction — written only in English, with Chinese translation long refused: her essays argue that Chinese carries memories she chooses not to revisit, making English 'a private language', inherited by no one and therefore chosen. From A Thousand Years of Good Prayers through the essays on depression and reading to Where Reasons End — conversations with a son lost to suicide — she reached the summit of English prose style in a second language (MacArthur 'genius' grant included). For anyone rebuilding their conceptual world in English, she is the deepest fellow-traveller text available.
