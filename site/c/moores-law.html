🔊 Moore's law /mʊrz lɔ/

英特尔创始人摩尔1965年观察:集成电路上的晶体管数量约每两年翻一番,计算能力指数级增长而成本骤降。它既是半导体行业的自我实现预言,也是过去半世纪数字革命的底层引擎——指数增长如何重塑一切的范本。

Intel co-founder Gordon Moore's 1965 observation that the number of transistors on a chip roughly doubles every two years — computing power growing exponentially as cost collapses. Both a self-fulfilling prophecy for the semiconductor industry and the underlying engine of the digital revolution — the exemplar of how exponential growth reshapes everything.

Etymology 词源

以 Gordon Moore 命名——非物理定律而是经验趋势,却因成为行业目标而自我实现数十年。
