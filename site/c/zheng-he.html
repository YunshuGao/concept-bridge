郑和下西洋 Zhèng Hé xià XīyángZheng He's voyages to the Western Ocean

🔊 expedition /ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən/

⏳ 1405年–1433年

15世纪初明朝国力雄厚，明成祖派郑和（回族宦官“三宝太监”）率船队出使西洋，主要目的“示中国富强”、提高明朝威望，兼以货物换取海外奇珍。1405–1433年七下西洋：船队最多200余艘、首航27000多人，宝船为当时世界最大最先进海船，编队严整、通信有法；先后到达亚非30多国，最远达非洲东海岸和红海沿岸，慷慨送礼发展友好、互通贸易，多国遣使随船来华。时间之长规模之大为世界航海史空前壮举，开创西太平洋与印度洋间的亚非海上交通线。（东南亚“三宝”地名庙宇犹存。）

Between 1405 and 1433 the Muslim eunuch admiral Zheng He led seven armadas — two hundred ships, twenty-seven thousand men, treasure junks dwarfing anything then afloat — through Southeast Asia to India, Arabia and the East African coast: showing the flag ('displaying China's wealth and power'), exchanging gifts and goods, ferrying foreign embassies home to Nanjing. The greatest voyages of the pre-modern world — and then, famously, they simply stopped, the fleets rotting while Iberian caravels a tenth the size set out the other way.

expedition 源自拉丁 expedire（解开脚镣→放行、出征）——舰队远征之词根妙合。

In the wild 语境例句

Zheng He v. Columbus is world history's favourite size contrast — a fleet of floating palaces sixty years before three small ships — and 'why China turned inward' its favourite exam question; Beijing's answer, rehearsed at every maritime forum, is that Zheng He traded where Europeans conquered.

Nuance 陷阱与辨析

宝船 = treasure ships；下西洋的“西洋”指今文莱以西海域（非 the West）。“和平之旅”叙事是当代中国外交的固定修辞（郑和=和平崛起的先证）——读 Economist 中国版会反复遇到并被审视。与 [[age-of-discovery]]、[[columbus]] 的对照是本节点的全部价值所在。
