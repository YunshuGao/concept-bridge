机会成本 jīhuì chéngběnopportunity cost

🔊 opportunity cost /ˌɑpərˈtunəti kɔst/

选择一件事所放弃的、次优选择本可带来的价值——真正的成本不是你付出的钱,而是你因此错过的最好替代方案。它是经济学最重要的一课:每个'是'都是对无数个'否'的宣判,理性决策比较的是机会成本而非账面支出。

The value of the best alternative given up when a choice is made — the true cost of anything is not the money you spend but the best alternative you forgo. It is economics' most important lesson: every yes is a sentence passed on countless nos, and rational decisions compare opportunity costs, not accounting outlays.

Etymology 词源

opportunity 源自拉丁 ob portum（顺风向港）——机会本义'顺风';机会成本即'为抓一个顺风而放弃的另一个'。

Synonyms 近义词

    The road not taken is the real price tag economics insists you read.
  • ▸ the next-best alternative /ðə nɛkst-bɛst ɔlˈtərnətɪv/
    The next-best alternative is what you actually pay when you choose.

The cost of a year in graduate school isn't just tuition — it's the salary forgone, the opportunity cost that the sticker price conveniently hides.
